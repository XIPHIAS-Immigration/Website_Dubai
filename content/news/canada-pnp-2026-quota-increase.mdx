## Canada Raises PNP Quotas for 2026
The Canadian federal government has announced a **significant increase in Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) quotas for 2026**, raising the total to **91,500 admissions**, up from 55,000 in 2025. At the same time, the Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP) intake has been reduced, reflecting a shift in Canada’s immigration priorities toward **permanent skilled migration**.

## Impact on Skilled Migrants

The increase in PNP allocations benefits **skilled professionals, entrepreneurs, and international graduates** seeking permanent residency in Canada. By focusing on PNP streams, provinces can select candidates with **specific skills aligned with regional labor market needs**, providing faster pathways to PR for eligible applicants.

Conversely, the scaling back of TFWP intake may affect **short-term work opportunities**, particularly for employers relying on temporary skilled workers from abroad. Migrants planning to enter Canada via work permits may need to **explore alternative options or provincial streams**.
