# Canada Targets H-1B Visa Holders as U.S. Tightens Immigration Rules — New Fast-Track Pathway for Skilled Workers

**Ottawa:** Canada is making a bold move to attract top global talent — especially **H-1B visa holders and skilled professionals** currently based in the United States.  
As the U.S. tightens its visa policies and increases fees, Canada is stepping in with a new **accelerated immigration pathway** to bring experienced researchers, tech workers, and innovators north of the border.  

This initiative comes as part of **Prime Minister Mark Carney’s** first federal budget, which earmarks **$1.2 billion (₹106 crore)** to recruit more than 1,000 international researchers and professionals to boost innovation and competitiveness.

> “The expertise of these researchers will help advance our global competitiveness and contribute to the economy of the future,” stated the Canadian government’s 2025 budget document.

---

## A Strategic Shift Toward High-Skilled Immigration

The new program is expected to offer a **fast-track route** for **H-1B visa holders in the U.S.**, who are facing longer processing times, rising fees, and uncertainty around green card policies under the Trump administration.  

Following the U.S. decision to **raise H-1B visa fees to $100,000**, many skilled immigrants — particularly from India — have been exploring relocation options. Canada’s new pathway provides a **stable alternative** with smoother access to **work permits and permanent residency**.

---

## Immigration Plan Balances Growth and Sustainability

While Canada is actively inviting high-skilled professionals, it’s also **tightening temporary and student visa numbers** to manage population pressures and housing shortages.  

Under the **2026–2028 Immigration Plan**, Ottawa is expected to maintain about **380,000 permanent residents per year**, but reduce **temporary residents** to 385,000 in 2026 and 370,000 by 2028 — a drop of over 40% compared to this year.

**Study permits** are also set to decline sharply — from **305,900 to 150,000** by 2027 — as the government focuses on sustainable growth in education and housing.

---

## Why Canada Is Reducing Temporary Visas

According to **Desjardins Financial Group**, the reduction in temporary residents is designed to stabilize wages and curb rental inflation.  
The report notes that slower population growth could help **balance Canada’s GDP per capita** and **ease housing demand**, especially in urban centers like Toronto and Vancouver.

Meanwhile, the government aims to bring the share of **non-permanent residents down to below 5%** of the total population by 2027, compared to 7.3% in mid-2025.

## What It Means for H-1B Holders and Skilled Professionals  

For thousands of **H-1B visa holders in the U.S.**, Canada’s initiative offers a chance to move to a country that values skilled expertise and provides a **clearer, faster route to permanent residency**.  

If you’re a professional in tech, research, or STEM fields, this could be your **best opportunity to transition** from temporary status in the U.S. to **permanent settlement in Canada**.
