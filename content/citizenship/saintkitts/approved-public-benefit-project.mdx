### Overview
Learn how St. Kitts & Nevis Citizenship by Investment can be obtained through a contribution to an approved Public Benefit Project (PBO), offering a fast, family-friendly route.

### Investment Overview
The PBO route requires a **minimum non-refundable contribution of USD 250,000** to a CIU-approved Public Benefit Project. This option supports national development while providing citizenship for the main applicant and eligible family members.

### Quick Eligibility Check
Check if you meet key criteria: clean criminal record, verifiable source of funds, eligible dependents, and no sanctions or restricted nationality issues.

### Featured Real Estate Projects
Not applicable for PBO; this route does **not involve real estate investment**. Focus is on impactful contributions to approved projects.

### How the Investment Route Works
1. Engage a licensed agent.  
2. Complete document collection and due diligence.  
3. Receive Approval-in-Principle (AIP).  
4. Make the PBO contribution.  
5. Take the oath, receive certificate, and apply for the St. Kitts & Nevis passport.

### Comparison of PBO, SISC, and Real Estate Routes {#comparison-with-provincial-entrepreneur-programs}
The St. Kitts & Nevis CBI program offers three main investment pathways. The Public Benefit Option (PBO) is a **donation-based route** focused on supporting approved projects. SISC is also donation-based, while the Real Estate option is **asset-backed**, providing a tangible investment with resale opportunities. All routes offer **fast processing (3–6 months), full family inclusion, and visa-free travel to over 150 countries**.

### Investment Risks and Compliance
- Non-disclosure of material information may result in rejection or revocation.  
- All applicants undergo strict KYC, AML, and CTF checks.  
- PBO route is regulated by the CIU to ensure legal and compliance standards.

### Why St. Kitts & Nevis
- No residency requirement; lifetime and inheritable citizenship.  
- Fast 3–6 month processing.  
- Visa-free or visa-on-arrival travel to over 150 countries.  
- Transparent, well-regulated program with family inclusion.

### FAQ
- **Can I include my family?** Yes, spouse, children under 26, and qualifying parents/grandparents 55+.  
- **Do I need to reside in St. Kitts & Nevis?** No, residency is not required.  
- **What is the minimum investment?** USD 250,000 contribution to an approved Public Benefit Project.

### Why Choose This Route
Ideal for applicants seeking a **non-real estate path**, supporting social and public benefit projects while securing citizenship.
