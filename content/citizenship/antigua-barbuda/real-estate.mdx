### Overview
The Real Estate route provides a straightforward path to Antigua & Barbuda citizenship. Investors can acquire government-approved property, maintain ownership for the required five-year period, and include eligible family members such as spouses, children, parents, or siblings under a single application.

### Investment Overview
Invest in approved real estate starting from USD 300,000 and retain ownership for five years before any sale. Standard processing, government, and due diligence fees apply and are scaled according to family size.

### Comparison with Entrepreneur Programs
Unlike entrepreneur or start-up visas that require active management, job creation, and ongoing presence, Antigua & Barbuda’s real estate route is primarily a capital-based, passive investment. The program offers clear holding requirements and full compliance with all citizenship by investment regulations.

---
### Investment Overview
The minimum entry for Antigua & Barbuda Real Estate citizenship is USD 300,000 in an approved project, plus due diligence, processing, and related government fees. A five-year minimum holding period applies before eligible exit or resale under program rules.

Ownership formats
- Share units — fractional stakes in approved developments; simpler entry and resale mechanics in many projects.
- Freehold title — deeded units at higher ticket sizes; suitable for those seeking personal use where permitted by the project.

Cash flow and exit
- Rental participation (if offered) and yields are not guaranteed; outcomes depend on operator performance and allocation policies.
- Exit is subject to project-specific resale windows and CIU rules in effect at the time of sale, following the five-year holding period.

Safeguards and compliance
- Closings use escrow and government-approved documentation such as SPA or title transfer through licensed agents and developers.
- All adult applicants undergo government due diligence; fees apply in addition to the investment.

Indicative budgeting
Many families plan USD 350,000 or more in liquid funds to cover the property stake, CIU processing, government charges, and third-party or closing costs. Costs vary by family size and project.
