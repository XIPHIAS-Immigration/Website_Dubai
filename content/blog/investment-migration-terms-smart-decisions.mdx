## Introduction

In a world where opportunities don’t stop at national borders, **investment migration** offers access to better lifestyles, safer environments, and broader business opportunities. However, the industry uses many specialized terms like **Citizenship by Investment (CBI)**, **Residency by Investment (RBI)**, **Golden Visa**, and **Due Diligence**. Understanding these terms is crucial for smarter decisions.

## Core Concepts of Investment Migration

### Citizenship by Investment (CBI)

A **CBI program** allows investors to gain full **citizenship** of another country by making a financial contribution, such as buying real estate, investing in government bonds, or donating to national funds.

**Why it matters:**
- Grants the right to **live, work, and study** in the country.
- Provides **visa-free or visa-on-arrival access** to multiple countries.
- Offers a **second passport** for global mobility.

**Popular destinations:** Antigua & Barbuda, St. Kitts & Nevis, Dominica, St. Lucia, Grenada, Turkey.

### Residency by Investment (RBI) / Golden Visa

**RBI programs**, often called **Golden Visas**, allow investors to gain **residency rights** through qualifying investments. Citizenship may follow after several years.

**Popular destinations:** Portugal, Greece, UAE.

### Second Passport

A **second passport** enhances **freedom of movement**, protects wealth, and provides access to global education and healthcare. It also acts as a **safety net**.

### Investment Options

Programs include investments in **real estate, businesses, government funds**, or approved **financial instruments**. Each option has different benefits and requirements.

### HNWI & HNI

High-Net-Worth Individuals (HNWI) and High-Income Individuals (HNI) are typical applicants.

- **Financial Capacity:** Sufficient assets or income to meet thresholds.
- **Global Opportunities:** Access programs in multiple countries.
- **Wealth Management:** Diversify investments internationally.
- **Family Benefits:** Dependents can benefit from migration programs.

### Citizenship Renunciation

Some programs require **renouncing current citizenship**, affecting legal and tax status. Applicants must weigh benefits carefully.

### Biometric Requirements

Applicants may provide **fingerprints, facial scans, or iris scans** to verify identity.

## Legal and Compliance Terms

### Due Diligence

Governments perform strict **background checks**, verifying identity and financial records to ensure funds are legitimate.

### AML (Anti-Money Laundering) & KYC (Know Your Customer)

- **AML** ensures funds are from legal sources.
- **KYC** requires proof of identity, financial history, and source of wealth.

These maintain transparency and protect both applicants and host countries.

## Mobility and Travel-Related Terms

### Global Mobility

Ability to move easily across borders for business, education, or lifestyle. Investment migration improves mobility through **visa-free travel**.

### Visa-Free Travel

Entry to countries without a prior visa, often granted via **CBI or RBI programs**.

### Passport Power

Measures how many countries a passport holder can enter without a visa. Top passports include Malta and Portugal.

## Emerging Trends in Investment Migration

### Digital Nomad Visa

Allows professionals to **live in one country while earning income remotely**. Early adopters include Estonia, Croatia, Spain, and Germany.

### Sustainable Investment

Some countries link programs to **renewable energy or cultural projects**, appealing to socially conscious investors.

## Why Understanding These Terms Matters

Understanding key terms helps investors:
- Choose programs that match their goals.
- Avoid compliance risks like **due diligence, AML, and KYC** violations.
- Maximize opportunities for **mobility, wealth planning, and family security**.

## FAQs

**What is investment migration?**
It’s obtaining **residency or citizenship** in another country through approved financial investments.

**What’s the difference between CBI and RBI?**
**CBI** grants citizenship immediately, while **RBI** offers residency that may lead to citizenship later.

**What is a Golden Visa?**
A **residency program** allowing investors to live in a country through financial investment.

**Why is due diligence important?**
Ensures only **credible applicants** are approved, protecting both investors and host countries.

**What does passport power mean?**
Measures how many countries a **passport holder** can enter without a visa.
