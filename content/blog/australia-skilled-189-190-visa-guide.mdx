## Australia PR Visa: Skilled Independent Sub Class 189

The **Australia Skilled Independent Sub Class 189 visa** is a permanent residency visa for skilled professionals in demand within Australia. This points-based visa allows candidates to live and work anywhere in Australia, and include eligible family members in a single application.

Applicants must demonstrate:

- English language proficiency
- Relevant employment experience
- Qualifications in an occupation on the Skilled Occupation List

This visa is ideal for skilled workers **without employer, state, or family sponsorship**. Applications are submitted through the [SkillSelect Expression of Interest (EOI)](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/australia-immigration-visa-consultants.html).

### Requirements for Sub Class 189:

- Submit an Australia Expression of Interest (EOI)
- Have work experience in a skilled occupation
- Positive skills assessment from relevant authority
- Age below 50 at the time of invitation
- Meet English language requirements
- Minimum 60 points on the points test
- Fulfill character and health requirements

## Skilled Nominated Sub Class 190 Visa

The **Skilled Nominated Sub Class 190 visa** is a permanent residency visa for skilled workers nominated by an Australian state or territory. Points-based, it requires applicants to live and work in the nominating state, while allowing inclusion of eligible family members.

Applicants must:

- Submit an EOI and receive nomination from a state/territory
- Work in an occupation listed on the State Skilled Occupation List
- Have a positive skills assessment
- Be under 50 years at the time of invitation
- Meet English language requirements
- Achieve minimum 60 points on the points test
- Fulfill character and health requirements
- Meet settlement funds and state employment obligations

Both visas provide pathways to permanent residency and eventual eligibility for Australian citizenship.

## FAQs

1. **What is the difference between Sub Class 189 and Sub Class 190 visas?**
   Sub Class 189 is independent and does not require state or employer sponsorship, while Sub Class 190 requires nomination from an Australian state or territory.

2. **Can family members be included in both 189 and 190 visa applications?**
   Yes, eligible family members can be included in both visa applications.

3. **What is the minimum points required to apply for these visas?**
   A minimum of 60 points is required to be considered for both Sub Class 189 and Sub Class 190 visas.
