## What is a Leasehold property?

Whilst a **leasehold** property physically belongs to the purchaser, the purchaser does however have rights over the land it is constructed on. The land in this case is owned by the freeholder or the landlord who leases the property to the buyer for **99 years (or less)**, the lease term can be extended upon expiry. There are many options to buy a **leasehold** property in **Dubai**.

## What is a Freehold property?

**Sheik Maktoum** introduced the **freehold** property ownership law in the year **2002**, this law allows non-**GCC** foreigners to purchase and legally own the physical property and also the land it is built on. The purchaser is granted the title deed of the property by the **Dubai Land Department (DLD)**. The freeholder is given the right to **occupy**, **lease** or **sell** the property on their will. In the event of the demise of the freeholder, the property can be **inherited** by their family members.

## Leasehold or Freehold—Which investment will allow an investor to obtain the Golden Visa of Dubai?

Although an investor looking at **ROI** from a real estate investment in **Dubai** can either purchase a **leasehold** or **freehold** property, to attain a **Golden Visa**, an investor **must invest in a freehold property**. One cannot apply for a **Golden Visa** if the property is purchased not under the visa guidelines. The **minimum real estate investment** required for getting a **Golden Visa** is **AED 1 million** or more, the visa guidelines also stipulate that an investor applying for a **Golden Visa** must procure properties from developers who are **approved by the DLD** and **before the construction is complete a purchase from a secondary market will not be eligible** as an investment for **Golden Visa**.

## What will be the validity of the Visa?

A visa is generally valid for **3 or 5 years** and this is dependent on the amount of investment made by a foreign buyer. The minimum investment of **AED 1 million** allows the grant of a **Golden Visa** for a period of **3 years**, and an investment of **AED 5 million** and above allows a **5-year** validity to the **Golden Visa**. And these can be **renewed** upon expiry irrespective of the amount invested.

No. To obtain the **Golden Visa**, the investment **must be in a freehold property**.
