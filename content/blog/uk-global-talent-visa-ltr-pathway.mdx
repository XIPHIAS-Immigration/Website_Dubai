After the exit of the **UK from the European Union** on 31st January 2020, the government introduced several immigration reforms to attract **highly skilled talent** and rejuvenate the UK economy. The **UK points-based system** was implemented to ensure that the right skills contribute actively to the economy and local communities.

The points-based system replaced the Tier 1 Visa program and caters to **skilled professionals, students, and specialist workers**. Each applicant needs to achieve a **minimum of 70 points** based on factors such as:

- Job offer by an approved sponsor
- Skill level
- English proficiency
- Current salary
- Job in a shortage occupation
- Educational qualifications

Applicants are categorized into three types: **Skilled Workers**, **Highly Skilled Workers**, and **Low Skilled Workers**. The **UK Global Talent Visa Program** is part of this system and allows individuals to gain permanent residency over time.

## Eligibility for UK Global Talent Visa
Applicants must be **18 years or older** and earn an endorsement from a recognized body, depending on their field:

- **Tech Nation**: Digital and tech fields
- **Royal Society / UKRI**: Science and research
- **Royal Academy of Engineering**: Engineering specialists
- **British Academy**: Humanities and social sciences
- **Arts Council England**: Arts specialists

Endorsements are categorized into:
- **Exceptional Talent**: For those who have already demonstrated significant achievements
- **Exceptional Promise**: For those with potential to become leaders in their field

The endorsement process typically takes **around 28 days** but may be extended if queries are raised by the endorsing body. After endorsement, applicants can apply for the **Global Talent Visa**, with the process usually taking **6–8 weeks**.

## Visa Validity and Leave to Remain (LTR)
The **Global Talent Visa** is valid for **5 years** and can be renewed. After establishing themselves in the UK, visa holders can apply for **Leave to Remain (LTR)**, which is equivalent to a **permanent residency**, granting long-term rights similar to a Green Card in the USA.

## FAQs

**1. What is the UK Global Talent Visa?**
- A route for **highly skilled individuals** to live and work in the **UK** with endorsement from a **recognized body** such as **Tech Nation, Royal Society, UKRI, Arts Council England, British Academy, or Royal Academy of Engineering**.

**2. Who can endorse my application?**
- **Tech Nation** (digital/tech)
- **Royal Society / UKRI** (science/research)
- **Royal Academy of Engineering** (engineering)
- **British Academy** (humanities/social sciences)
- **Arts Council England** (arts)

**3. How long does endorsement take?**
- Usually **28 days**, may take longer if queries arise.

**4. How long does the whole process take?**
- After endorsement, the **Global Talent Visa** process takes about **6–8 weeks**.

**5. Does the Global Talent Visa lead to LTR (Leave to Remain)?**
- Yes, the visa is valid for **5 years** and can be renewed. After demonstrating value in the UK, applicants can apply for **Leave to Remain (LTR)**.

**6. What is the difference between Exceptional Talent and Exceptional Promise?**
- **Exceptional Talent**: Already demonstrated significant achievements
- **Exceptional Promise**: Potential to become leaders in their field

**7. Is there an age requirement?**
- Applicants must be **18 years or older**
