On **June 3, 2019**, the province’s immigrant nominee program announced that **British Columbia’s** **BC PNP Tech Pilot** would continue until **June 2020**.

Introduced in **2017**, the **Tech Pilot** provides a fast-tracked process to obtain **Canadian Permanent Residence** for international talent with a **job offer** in one of **29 eligible tech occupations**.


## BC PNP Streams

The **Tech Pilot** is **not** a separate stream of the **BC PNP**. Applicants must apply under an existing **BC PNP** stream to be considered for the **Tech Pilot**, including:

- **Skills Immigration**
- **Express Entry BC**
- **Entrepreneur Immigration**
- **Entrepreneur Immigration — Regional Pilot**

### Job Requirements for BC PNP Tech Pilot

Applicants under **Skills Immigration** must have a confirmed **job offer** from a **BC employer**, ideally in one of the **29 key technology occupations**.  

- Job offer must be **full-time**, for **at least one year**, and have **≥120 days** remaining at application.  
- **Permanent** job offers or those without an end date are also accepted.


## BC PNP Draws

After registering online with the **BC PNP**, candidates receive a score based on selection criteria.  

- Applicants with the **highest scores** receive an **Invitation to Apply (ITA)**.  
- Registrants have **30 days** to submit their nomination application and pay a **CAD $700** fee.  


## BC PNP Processing Time

- Most applications are processed within **2–3 months**.  
- **Tech Pilot** applications often receive **priority processing**.  
- Approved nominees can apply for **Permanent Residence** with **IRCC** under the **Provincial Nominee Class**.  
- Some candidates may work on a **temporary work permit** in **British Columbia** during the PR process.


## FAQs

**1) What is the BC PNP Tech Pilot?**  
The **BC PNP Tech Pilot** fast-tracks **BC PNP** applications for candidates with a qualifying **job offer** in one of **29 eligible tech occupations**, accelerating their path to **Canadian Permanent Residence**.

**2) Do I need a job offer to qualify?**  
Yes. A **full-time job offer** from a **BC employer** for **at least one year** with **≥120 days** remaining is required, ideally in an **eligible tech occupation**.

**3) Which BC PNP streams work with the Tech Pilot?**  
The **Tech Pilot** operates through existing **BC PNP** streams such as **Skills Immigration** and **Express Entry BC**; it is **not** a separate stream.

**4) How long does processing take and what are the fees?**  
Typical **BC PNP** processing is **2–3 months**, with **Tech Pilot** cases often faster. The **provincial application fee** is **CAD $700**. (Times and fees may change.)
