Toronto has been named one of the world’s best cities for start-up workers, thanks to its salaries and comparatively low cost of living which can come back as a surprise to some residents. The list, released by accommodation provider Nestpick, rated 85 cities on their start-up ecosystem standing, as well as other indicators like salaries, cost of living, vacation days, and gender equality. - Toronto came in tenth, with the list topped by Singapore, otherwise dominated by European cities. Helsinki, Finland placed second, followed by the tech hub San Francisco.
- The list was rounded out by Berlin (4); Copenhagen, Denmark (5); Tel Aviv, Israel (6); Zurich, Switzerland (7); Seoul, South Korea (8) and Munich, Germany (9).
- Toronto placed third for its wage score, behind San Francisco and Zurich, fifth for cost of living and quality of life, and sixth for the start-up ecosystem.

Nestpick, which helps professionals find accommodation after relocating for work, said start-ups seem to be the biggest reason for clients moving to larger cities—and companies’ attractiveness to talent is supported not just by pay, but also livability and other offerings in their city.

Certain cities could offer higher salaries, but when factoring taxes and living expenses, the net benefit may not be as high, says Nestpick’s spokesperson.

Similarly, professionals should consider quality of life: are vacation days adequate? Is healthcare accessible as you would like?
