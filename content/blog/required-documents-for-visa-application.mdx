## Common Required Documents for Visa Application

Here’s a list of documents that are usually needed for most **visa applications** around the world:

## 1. Passport

Your **passport** is the main form of identification for any **visa application**.

- **Why it’s needed:** It proves who you are and your nationality.
- **How to prepare:** Make sure your **passport** is valid for at least 6–12 months. Keep copies of all important pages handy.

## 2. Birth Certificate

A **birth certificate** shows your date and place of birth.

- **Why it’s needed:** Most **visas** require proof of identity and age.
- **How to prepare:** Obtain an official copy from your local government office. Some countries may ask for a certified copy.

## 3. Educational Certificates & Transcripts

If you’re applying for a **study visa** or **skilled migration visa**, your degrees, diplomas, and transcripts are significant.

- **Why it’s needed:** They prove your education and show that you meet **visa requirements**.
- **How to prepare:** Request official copies from your school or university. Some countries may also ask for notarized copies or certified translations.

## 4. Work Experience Letters / Reference Letters

Proof of your work history is usually needed for **employment-based visas**.

- **Why it’s needed:** Confirms your experience and skills for your job.
- **How to prepare:** Get letters on your company’s official letterhead, signed by HR or your manager. Include job title, period worked, and contact details.

## 5. Financial Documents

When applying for a **visa**, you need to show financial capability.

- **Why it’s needed:** Immigration authorities want assurance that you can cover living costs.
- **How to prepare:** Gather recent bank statements, tax returns, or salary slips. Ensure they are clear and up-to-date.

## 6. Police Clearance Certificate (PCC)

A **PCC** confirms whether you have a criminal record.

- **Why it’s needed:** Immigration departments ensure applicants have a clean legal record.
- **How to prepare:** Apply at your local police station or via official online process. Processing times vary.

## 7. Medical Certificates / Health Reports

Medical reports are required to confirm health status.

- **Why it’s needed:** Authorities ensure you do not have contagious diseases or major health concerns.
- **How to prepare:** Visit an approved medical center for check-ups and vaccinations. Keep copies for submission.

## 8. Marriage Certificate (if applicable)

For family or dependent **visas**, a **marriage certificate** may be required.

- **Why it’s needed:** Confirms marital status.
- **How to prepare:** Obtain an official copy from local authorities. Certified translations may be needed.

## 9. Passport-Size Photographs

- **Why it’s needed:** Used for identification and **visa processing**.
- **How to prepare:** Follow country-specific size, background, and format requirements. Ensure photos are recent and clear.

## 10. Resume / CV

May be required for **work visas** or **skilled migration visas**.

- **Why it’s needed:** Shows education, work experience, and skills.
- **How to prepare:** Keep concise, updated, and easy to read. Include relevant responsibilities and qualifications.

## Tips for Preparing Your Visa Documents

- **Keep copies of everything:** Have paper and digital copies.
- **Check translations or notarizations:** Some documents require translation or notarization.
- **Check expiration dates:** Ensure passports, bank statements, and certificates are valid.
- **Stay organized with a checklist:** Use a folder or digital file to track your documents.
