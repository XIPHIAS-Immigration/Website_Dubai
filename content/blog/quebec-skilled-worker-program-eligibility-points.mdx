[Quebec](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/canada-quebec-skilled-immigration.html) has today announced **drastic changes** to its Selection Criteria for the Quebec Skilled Worker Program. For a **single applicant**, the qualifying points will now be **50** (earlier 49) and for **married applicants** 59 (earlier 57). There has also been a considerable general drop in **Area of Training (AOT) points** for several specific fields of education and training. The maximum points available under AOT will be **12** (earlier 16), and most training which previously fetched 12 points, for example, nurses, will now get only 9 (earlier 12). These changes make it more difficult to achieve the points required to qualify.

The above changes may also affect your application, as Quebec has announced that these changes will also apply to applications submitted before 08 March 2017 where the preliminary examination has not yet started, including those submitted under **Mon Projet Quebec**.

### Key Changes in Eligibility Points

- **Single applicants:** 50 points (previously 49)
- **Married applicants:** 59 points (previously 57)
- **Area of Training (AOT) points:** max 12 points (previously 16)
- **Nursing & other high-demand fields:** now 9 points (previously 12)

### Impact on Applications

These changes may affect current and pending applications. Applicants under review may experience adjustments in scoring due to revised point allocations. Staying informed ensures proper planning for eligibility.
