## Introduction

The introduction of the **Skilled Visa Australia** has opened opportunities for individuals to **migrate and contribute to Australia’s growth**.  
The [Skilled Worker Visa in Australia](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/australia-immigration-visa-consultants.html) caters to specialists and skilled professionals, including those sponsored by employers or approved under a **State Migration Plan**, offering additional benefits.

Australia offers **four main immigration programs**, each with its own purpose and requirements. Understanding these programs ensures applicants meet eligibility criteria.

---

## Main Skilled Migration Programs

### 1. Skilled Migration Based on Points
- Candidates take a **points-based test**
- Applicants who meet the required points contribute to Australia’s economy

### 2. Permanent Employer Sponsored Program
- Sponsored by an **Australian employer**
- Eligible to migrate after completing immigration procedures

### 3. Business Investment & Innovation Program
- For business professionals with a **successful track record**
- Supports business growth and innovation in Australia

### 4. Recognized Talent Program
- For individuals with **exceptional skills**, including sports, music, and arts
- Eligible for migration based on international recognition

---

## Visa Types to Immigrate to Australia

### 1. Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189)
- Points-based system  
- No sponsorship needed  
- Work and live in Australia as **permanent residents**

### 2. Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190)
- Points-based with **sponsorship from a family member, employer, or government**  
- Work and live as permanent residents

### 3. Skilled Regional (Provisional) Visa (Subclass 489)
- Live and work in **designated regional areas**  
- Valid for **up to 4 years**  
- Requires an **invitation to apply**

### 4. Temporary Skilled Worker Visa (Subclass 457)
- Work in a nominated company for **up to 4 years**  
- Requires **company sponsorship**

### 5. Skilled Employer Nomination Scheme (Subclass 186)
- **Permanent visa** for skilled workers  
- Three streams: **Temporary Residence Transition**, **Direct Entry**, and **Agreement Stream**

### 6. Regional Sponsored Migration Scheme (Subclass 187)
- Sponsored by an **Australian company in regional areas**  
- Offers similar streams as Subclass 186

---

## Eligibility Criteria

To qualify for the Australia Skilled Migration Program, applicants must:

- Receive an **invitation from the Australian Government**
- Have an occupation listed on the **Skilled Occupations List**
- Achieve the required **points score**
- Demonstrate **English language proficiency**
- Be **under 50 years of age**

## FAQs

**1. What are the main Skilled Visa programs in Australia?**  
Subclass 189 (Independent), Subclass 190 (Nominated), Subclass 489 (Regional), Subclass 457 (Temporary), Subclass 186 (Employer Nomination), and Subclass 187 (Regional Sponsorship).

**2. Who is eligible for Skilled Migration?**  
Skilled professionals under 50 who meet the required points, English proficiency, and occupation criteria.

**3. Can employer sponsorship increase chances of approval?**  
Yes, sponsorship by an Australian employer, family member, or government can enhance eligibility.

**4. How long does it take to get permanent residency?**  
It depends on the visa type. Some visas offer **immediate permanent residency**, while others require a **temporary period first**.
