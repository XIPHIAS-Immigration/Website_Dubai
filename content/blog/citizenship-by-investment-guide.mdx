## Meaning of Citizenship

A **citizen** is a person legally recognized by a country, with rights such as **voting**, **working**, and **owning property**, and duties to **respect the nation’s laws**. Traditionally, citizenship is obtained **by birth**, **by naturalization** (after residence), or **through marriage**.

In the 1980s, a new pathway emerged: **Citizenship by Investment (CBI)**, also called **economic citizenship**.

## What Is Citizenship by Investment (CBI)?

Certain countries offer **citizenship** to **high-net-worth** applicants who make a **qualifying investment**. In return, investors often obtain a **second passport**, enhanced global mobility, and access to new markets. **International investments** via CBI have drawn growing interest worldwide.

## Why Countries Offer CBI (Government Perspective)

- **Foreign Direct Investment (FDI):** Funds **public** and **private** development (e.g., **Vanuatu** channels donations into national projects).
- **Business & Jobs:** Encourages entrepreneurs to **set up companies** and **create employment**.
- **Population Strategy:** Some countries (e.g., **Moldova**) use CBI to **attract new residents** and talent.

## Investor Benefits

- **Second passport & mobility:** Often **100+ visa-free/VOA destinations**, enabling faster travel and business access.
- **Global opportunity:** Expand markets and operations (e.g., **Caribbean CBI** opens regional and global gateways).
- **Tax efficiency:** Many programs reduce or exempt tax on **foreign-sourced income**; inheritance/wealth taxes may be minimal.
- **Dual citizenship:** Most CBI countries **allow dual nationality**.
- **Family inclusion:** Spouse and **dependent children** (and sometimes parents) can be added. **Dominica’s** framework even supports **larger families** under set conditions.
- **Education & lifestyle:** **Malta** connects investors to **EU** study/work options; island programs offer high-quality living environments.

> **Note:** Benefits, destinations, and tax rules vary by country—always review the latest official program criteria.

## Typical Eligibility & Documents

- **Age:** Usually **21+** for the main applicant.
- **Clean background:** **Police clearance** for all adults included.
- **Medical fitness:** **Health certificates** confirming good health.
- **Source of funds:** Documented, **lawful** origin of investment funds.
- **Program compliance:** Invest via **approved routes** and meet due-diligence requirements.

## Main Investment Routes

1. **Real Estate:** Pre-approved **hotels/resorts/residential** projects with a **minimum hold period**.
2. **Business/Enterprise:** Invest as a **passive** or **active** partner, sometimes with **job-creation** targets.
3. **National Funds/Donations:** Non-refundable contributions to government funds or **sovereign projects**.

## Notable Mentions from This Guide

- **Vanuatu — Citizenship by Investment:** Straightforward **donation** pathway supporting national development.
- **Moldova — CBI:** Aimed at attracting investors; citizenship can enhance mobility and opportunity.
- **Dominica — CBI:** Family-friendly structures; celebrated as the **“Nature Isle.”**
- **Malta — Investment Migration:** Access to the **EU** ecosystem for living, studying, and working.
- **Montenegro — CBI:** Requires applying **before the end of 2025**, with **approved real-estate** options.
