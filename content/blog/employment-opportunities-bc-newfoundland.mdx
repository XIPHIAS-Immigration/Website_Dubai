According to the **New Labor Force Survey**, **British Columbia** and **Newfoundland and Labrador** experienced the highest job growth in October 2019. Statistics Canada reported **443,000 new jobs** nationwide between October 2018 and October 2019.

The **national employment rate** remained steady at **5.5%**, while net employment fell by 1,800 jobs in October. Despite this, August and September saw significant employment gains.

## Regional Employment Highlights

**British Columbia**
- Added **15,000 new jobs** in October
- Unemployment rate remained at **4.7%**, the lowest in Canada

**Newfoundland and Labrador**
- Created **2,700 new jobs**
- Employment levels remained stable

### Employment Trends by Age Group

- **Workers over 55:** 31,000 new jobs; unemployment dropped to **4.7%**
- **Workers aged 24–54:** Net job increase of **94,000** compared to October 2018
- **Workers aged 15–24:** No significant change in employment

### Industry-wise Employment Changes

- **Construction:** Decreased by 21,000 jobs
- **Manufacturing:** Decreased by 23,000 jobs (mostly in Ontario)
- **Public Administration:** Increased by 20,000 jobs, with gains in **British Columbia, New Brunswick, Quebec, and Prince Edward Island**
- **Finance, Real Estate, Insurance, Rental, and Leasing:** Added 18,000 jobs
