**Canada Parents and Grandparents Program (PGP) 2023: Lottery Application Now Open**

From **October 10–23, 2023**, **Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)** plans to issue **24,200** **Invitations to Apply (ITAs)** to potential sponsors. IRCC aims to process and accept a maximum of **15,000** complete applications.

---

**PGP 2023 — How the Process Works**

When you receive an **Invitation to Apply (ITA)** through the **Parents and Grandparents Program (PGP)**, you must submit **two applications**:

- **Sponsorship application** (by the sponsor in **Canada**)
- **Permanent residence (PR)** application (for the parent/grandparent)

If both are approved by **IRCC**, you’ll sign an **undertaking** (financial support agreement). This undertaking **starts on the day** the person you’re sponsoring (and eligible family members) becomes a **permanent resident** of **Canada**.

> **Who can be invited in 2023?**
> You can sponsor your **parents or grandparents** in the **2023** lottery if you **submitted the interest to sponsor form in 2020** and **receive an ITA** for the 2023 intake. If you previously submitted the form but didn’t get an invitation in **2021** or **2022**, watch your **email (and spam folder)** for an ITA from **IRCC**.

**Update for Applicants Looking to Settle in Canada**

Under **Canada’s Immigration Levels Plan 2023–2025**, the targets include welcoming **465,000** new permanent residents in 2023, with **28,500** **parents and grandparents**. By **2025**, the plan targets **500,000** newcomers, including **36,000** **parents and grandparents**.

**FAQs**

**1) Do I need to have submitted the interest to sponsor form in 2020?**
Yes. For the **PGP 2023** intake, **IRCC** is inviting from the **2020 interest to sponsor** pool. Only those who **receive an ITA** can apply.

**2) How will I receive my invitation?**
Invitations are sent by **email** from **IRCC**. Check your **inbox and spam/junk folder** during the **October 10–23, 2023** window.

**3) What is the undertaking period for PGP sponsorship?**
Sponsors sign a legally binding **undertaking** to financially support the sponsored person for a **long-term period** (commonly **20 years**; **Quebec** has its own rules). The clock starts once **PR** is granted.

**4) I didn’t get an ITA—what are my options?**
You can consider other **family sponsorship** options (e.g., **Super Visa** for extended stays) or prepare for future **PGP** intakes while maintaining eligibility (income, tax filings, and documents).
