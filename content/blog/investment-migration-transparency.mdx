## What Investment Migration Really Means
**Investment migration** provides individuals the opportunity to gain **residency** or **citizenship** in another country by making a financial investment that supports the host nation’s economic growth. Programs usually fall under two main types—**Citizenship by Investment (CBI)** or **Residency by Investment (RBI)**.

However, as these programs become more popular, so do the challenges. Without clear information or reliable guidance, applicants can face unnecessary delays or legal issues. This is why following proper **investment migration standards** is so important for a smooth experience.

## FAQs
**Q1. What is the difference between CBI and RBI in investment migration?**
A. **Citizenship by Investment (CBI)** grants **citizenship** after a qualifying **investment**, while **Residency by Investment (RBI)** provides **residency rights** (and potential future citizenship) based on **investment** and **compliance** with program rules.

**Q2. Why do governments emphasize due diligence for investors?**
A. Robust **due diligence** protects program integrity by verifying **source of funds**, **background**, and **documentation**, reducing **fraud** risks and ensuring **international compliance** standards are met.

**Q3. How can transparency from consultants help my application?**
A. Clear disclosures on **fees**, **timelines**, **eligibility**, and **document checklists** prevent mistakes, avoid surprises, and build **trust**, resulting in a smoother **approval** process.

**Q4. What documents should I prepare before starting an investment migration application?**
A. Typically: **passport**, **police clearance**, **bank statements**, **proof of funds/source of wealth**, **investment agreements**, and any **legal/financial records** required by the specific **CBI/RBI** program.
