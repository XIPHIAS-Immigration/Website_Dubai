Due to the aging population, the Govt of **New Brunswick Provincial Nominee Program (NBPNP)** is intending to welcome more migrants to the province for the next five years. Hence, it has targeted to accept **7,500** candidates each year to achieve the numbers by the end of **2024**. Undoubtedly, it accepted that this target will expand the number of residents in the region by **1%** and makes two times the proportion of **migrants** who are already staying in the province.

## Program Overview & Population Strategy (NBPNP + AIP)

Their population strategy clearly states that achieving immigration targets would be conceivable, through the **NBPNP** and **AIP**. Under the **New Brunswick Provincial Nominee Program (NBPNP)**, the province can decide the eligible group of **businessmen/entrepreneurs**, **skilled workers**, and **international graduates** from post-secondary institutes of **New Brunswick** to apply for **Canada PR**. In addition, the **Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)** is a significant employer-driven initiative established in the **Atlantic Canada** region last year to hire international workers to provide labor needs.

## Key Goals of the New Brunswick PNP (Next 5 Years)

- **Employ entrepreneurs/businessmen** to improve sustainable **economic development**.
- Achieve a **spirited/energetic settlement environment** so that new **immigrants** can successfully settle in the province with their families.
- **Enhance communities** to accomplish openness to **immigrants** and expand further.
- **Draw skilled workers** to meet evolving **labor market** requirements.

All the above objectives are fundamentally proposed to attract the **business network**, **international workers**, and **skilled workers**.

## Additional Support Objectives

- Support **cultural capability training** for companies in **New Brunswick**.
- **Link groups and communities** to support newcomers in their exploratory visit.
- Assist **immigrant settlement agencies** to offer services meeting the requirements of a new immigrant’s **spouse and children**.
- Look into the **Entrepreneur** group of **NBPNP** to guarantee that new immigrants can successfully **set up their business**.
- **Support international students** to settle in the province after education and help provincial immigration programs in **francophone** markets.

## FAQs

**How many newcomers is New Brunswick targeting each year via NBPNP?**
The province is targeting **7,500 candidates annually** through **2024** to address **population aging** and labor needs.

**Who can be nominated under NBPNP?**
**Entrepreneurs/business owners**, **skilled workers**, and **international graduates** from **New Brunswick** post-secondary institutions may be selected to apply for **Canada PR**.

**How does AIP differ from NBPNP?**
The **Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)** is **employer-driven** for the Atlantic region, while **NBPNP** is the province’s **nominee program** that selects candidates across streams (e.g., **skilled**, **business**, **graduate**).

**What community supports are emphasized?**
The plan highlights **cultural training**, **community linkages**, **settlement services** for families, **entrepreneur support**, and pathways for **international students**—all aimed at **successful settlement**.
