## Key Benefits of Caribbean CBI

1. **Family inclusion:** Add your **spouse** and **dependent children** for an additional fee. Future generations may obtain citizenship through **naturalization**.  
2. **Visa-free mobility:** Caribbean passports allow **visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to 100+ countries**, including the **UK**, **Singapore**, and most of the **Schengen Area**.  
3. **Tax advantages:** Many islands have **favorable tax regimes**—no tax on foreign-sourced income and minimal inheritance or wealth taxes.  
4. **Fast processing:** Application processing typically takes **2–4 months**, subject to due diligence.  
5. **Dual citizenship & privacy:** Most programs **permit dual citizenship** and maintain **confidential** application processing.  
6. **No residency requirement:** You and your family are **not required to live** in the country.  
7. **Lifestyle appeal:**  
   - **Dominica** – “The Nature Isle,” known for rainforests and waterfalls.  
   - **Antigua & Barbuda** – Home to **365 beaches**.  
   - **Grenada** – The “**Spice Isle**,” offering both beauty and business advantages.  
8. **Safety & environment:** Caribbean nations maintain **low pollution** and relatively **safe environments**.  
9. **Nation-building:** Your investment contributes to **public infrastructure** and **private-sector development**.  
10. **U.S. market access via Grenada:** **Grenada citizenship** can support eligibility for the **U.S. E-2 Treaty Investor Visa**, subject to U.S. policy and adjudication.

## Eligibility — Typical CBI Standards

To qualify for most Caribbean CBI programs, the **main applicant** must:

- Be **21 years or older**  
- Hold a **clean criminal record** (verified via police clearance)  
- Make the **required qualifying investment** (donation or real estate)  
- Submit **medical certificates** showing good health  
- Comply with each country’s **approved investment pathways**

## FAQs

**1) Donation or real estate — which is better?**  
**Donation** routes are generally **simpler and quicker** with lower upfront costs. **Real estate** options offer a **tangible asset** and potential returns but require a **minimum holding period** and carry **market risk**.

**2) Do I need to visit the country?**  
In most cases, **no visit is required** to obtain citizenship. Some programs may request a brief **oath ceremony** or in-person attendance during renewals.

**3) Can my parents be included?**  
Yes, many programs allow **dependent parents or grandparents**, provided they meet **age and dependency** requirements.

**4) How fast can I get the passport?**  
With complete documentation and successful due diligence, approvals typically take **2–4 months**. Timelines may vary by country and applicant background.

**5) Does Grenada really help with a U.S. E-2 Visa?**  
**Yes.** **Grenada** is an **E-2 treaty country**, meaning its citizens **can apply** for a **U.S. E-2 Investor Visa**. However, approval remains at the **discretion of U.S. authorities**.
