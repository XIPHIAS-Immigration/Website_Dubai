The **United States EB-5 visa program** is a popular and effective way for **high-net-worth individuals and their families** to obtain a **Green Card**. Designed to attract **foreign investment** and **create jobs** for American citizens, the **EB-5 program** is one of the **fastest routes** to permanent residency in the United States.

Once the **investor's petition** is approved, investors and their families can obtain a **conditional Green Card** within months. The U.S. is a **diverse**, **multi-religious democracy** with a **robust economy**, making it an attractive destination for Indians seeking the **American Dream**.

## Pathways under the US EB-5 Visa Program

There are **two pathways** to an EB-5 Immigrant Investor Visa:

- **Direct EB-5 Investment**: Requires an investment in a **real operating business** that creates **10 full-time jobs**. I-526 approval is generally **faster** than through a Regional Center. Investors can **withdraw funds** after business obligations are met.
- **Regional Center EB-5**: Investments are pooled and can count **indirect job creation**. Typically requires a **5–9 year lock-in period**.

## Benefits of a Green Card

- **Renewable** every **10 years**.
- **Travel flexibility** to and from the U.S.
- **Freedom to live anywhere** in the United States.
- Eligible for **Social Security**, **education assistance**, and **government financial aid**.
- Greater **job opportunities**, including positions requiring **security clearances** or **government employment**.

## Requirements of the US EB-5

Investors must invest in a **new commercial enterprise** that was:

- Established **after 29th November 1990**; or
- Purchased **before 29th November 1990** but **restructured or expanded** to create a **new commercial enterprise** and **increase net worth/employees by 40%**.

The enterprise must be **for-profit** and lawfully conducting business.

### Minimum Investment Amounts

| **Filing Date** | **Minimum Investment** | **TEA (Targeted Employment Area)** | **High-Employment Area** |
| --- | ---:| ---:| ---:|
| Before 3/15/2022 | $500,000 | $500,000 | $1,000,000 |
| On or After 3/15/2022 | $800,000 | $800,000 (includes infrastructure projects) | $1,050,000 |

The **EB-5 Reform and Integrity Act of 2022** increased **accountability**, including:

- **New reporting requirements** for Regional Centers
- **USCIS site visits** to projects
- **Extended job-creation windows**
- **Stricter compliance and investor protections**
- More **innovative projects** with **better potential returns** and **6–7 year exit options**

## Benefits of the EB-5 Program

- **Fast processing times**: Conditional Green Card within months.
- **No sponsor required**: Ideal for entrepreneurs planning to **settle, work, and expand** in the U.S.
- **Investment flexibility**: Real estate, healthcare, technology, and more.
- **Path to US citizenship**: Apply for full citizenship after **5 years** of permanent residency.
- **Education and healthcare benefits**: Same as U.S. citizens.

Indian nationals are increasingly interested in EB-5 due to **H-1B backlogs**. Approximately **33% of immigrant-founded U.S. startups** have **Indian founders**, highlighting the program's relevance for entrepreneurs.

## FAQs

**Q1. Difference between Direct EB-5 and Regional Center EB-5?**
A. **Direct EB-5**: Invest in a **real business** and create **10 full-time W-2 jobs**.
**Regional Center EB-5**: Counts **indirect jobs**, pooled projects, **longer lock-in**, less control.

**Q2. Current minimum investment amounts?**
A. From **3/15/2022**: $800,000 in a **TEA**, $1,050,000 in **high-employment areas**. Prior thresholds: $500,000/$1,000,000.

**Q3. How does the EB-5 Reform and Integrity Act of 2022 help?**
A. Improves **transparency**, **compliance**, **site visits**, **extended job creation**, and offers **clearer exit options**, boosting **investor confidence**.
