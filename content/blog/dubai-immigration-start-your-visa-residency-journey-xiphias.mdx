Dubai's immigration procedures for tourists, residents, and workers are overseen by the **General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA)**. Dubai offers a range of visa categories including **Property Owner Visa, Tourist Visa, Work Visa, Investor Visa, Retiree Visa, Residency Visas (Green & Golden Visas)**, and work permits. Eligible nationalities can also benefit from **visa-on-arrival options**. For added convenience, Dubai Airports provides the **Smart Gate system** to expedite passport control.

## Dubai Business Visa

The **Dubai business visa** allows individuals to move to Dubai for professional purposes such as attending meetings, conferences, or exploring investment opportunities. Options include short-term visit visas (valid for 30 or 90 days) and longer-term multiple-entry visas valid for up to six months.

[Dubai working visa](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/dubai-business-investor-visa.html) allows foreign nationals to legally live, work, and settle in Dubai. These visas are typically arranged through **employer sponsorship** and follow UAE labor and immigration regulations.

## Dubai Residency & Golden Visa

Dubai offers several long-term **residency options**, including the **Golden Visa** and **Green Visa**. While the UAE does not offer traditional permanent residency, it provides extended residency for **skilled professionals, investors, entrepreneurs, and individuals with exceptional talents**.

The **Golden Visa** is available for 10 years to **property owners, investors, entrepreneurs, and exceptional talent contributors**. Eligibility includes investment in real estate, substantial capital investment, or notable humanitarian contributions.

The cost of living in Dubai is relatively high, especially rent. A single individual’s monthly expenses, excluding rent, are estimated at **AED 4,000 (USD 1,100)**.
