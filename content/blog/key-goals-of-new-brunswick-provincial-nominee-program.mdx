Due to an aging population, the **New Brunswick Provincial Nominee Program (NBPNP)** plans to welcome more migrants over the next five years. The program targets **7,500 candidates each year**, aiming to expand the province's population by 1% and double the proportion of migrants currently residing in New Brunswick.

The province's strategy to achieve these immigration targets relies on **NBPNP** and the **Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)**. NBPNP allows the province to select eligible **businessmen, skilled workers, and international graduates** from post-secondary institutions to apply for **Canada PR**. Additionally, the **AIP** is an employer-driven initiative to hire international workers and meet labor needs in Atlantic Canada.

## Key Goals of the New Brunswick Provincial Nominee Program

1. **Support Entrepreneurs and Businessmen**: Encourage sustainable economic growth through investment and business development.
2. **Facilitate Settlement for New Immigrants**: Create a supportive environment for newcomers and their families to integrate successfully.
3. **Strengthen Communities**: Promote inclusivity and openness within local communities for immigrants.
4. **Attract Skilled Workers**: Address labor market demands by attracting professionals in high-demand fields.
5. **Cultural Capability Training**: Support companies in enhancing workforce cultural competency.
6. **Community Linkages**: Connect newcomers with local groups and organizations to ease settlement.
7. **Immigrant Settlement Services**: Assist agencies in providing services for new immigrants and their families.
8. **Entrepreneur Support**: Ensure new immigrant entrepreneurs can successfully establish their businesses.
9. **Support International Students**: Facilitate retention of international graduates in the province and support francophone immigration programs.

## FAQs

**How many candidates does NBPNP plan to accept annually?**
The program targets **7,500 candidates each year** to meet its five-year immigration goals.

**Who is eligible for NBPNP?**
Eligible candidates include **businessmen, skilled workers, and international graduates** from New Brunswick's post-secondary institutions.

**What is the role of the Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)?**
AIP is an **employer-driven initiative** designed to hire international workers to meet labor demands in Atlantic Canada.

**Can international students settle in New Brunswick after graduation?**
Yes, the program supports **international students** in transitioning to permanent residency and contributing to the province's workforce.

**How does NBPNP support entrepreneurs?**
The program ensures that **new immigrant entrepreneurs** can establish their business successfully and contribute to economic growth.
