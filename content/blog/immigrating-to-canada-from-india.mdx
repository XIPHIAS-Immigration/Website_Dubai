## Immigration for Spouses

The **Canadian immigration policy** states that you can consider someone to be a **spouse/partner** when they meet the relationship definitions (e.g., **spouse** or **common-law**). You would **not** need to make a **separate application** for your partner when you make your own immigration application—**Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)** will assume you plan to migrate with your **spouse/partner** if you include them as a **dependent**. Make sure you know the process for **Canada Spouse Visa immigration** before you apply.

## Immigration for Children

Any **dependent** below the age of **22 years** who does **not** have a **spouse/partner** can be considered a **child** and will be eligible for all the benefits due to a child as per **IRCC** rules. In certain cases, if the dependent is **22+** but has a qualifying **physical or mental condition** that restricts them from working or fending for themselves, they can also be included in your general application as the **principal applicant’s dependent**. If your **spouse/partner** has children who meet these criteria, you can include them in your application as well.

## For Parents & Grandparents

At present, **IRCC** does **not** allow **parents and/or grandparents** to be part of the initial **Family Immigration** when applying with the principal applicant. They must be **sponsored separately** once the primary applicant has **settled in Canada** and met the **minimum residence**/income requirements to sponsor **parents and grandparents** as **PRs**.

## FAQs

**1) Can I include my spouse and children in my Canada PR application?**
**Answer:** Yes. You can include your **spouse/partner** and **dependent children** in the same application so your family can obtain **PR** together.

**2) What is the age limit for dependent children?**
**Answer:** Generally **under 22** and **unmarried/not in a common-law relationship**. Certain **over-22** dependents with qualifying conditions can also be included per **IRCC** rules.

**3) Can I bring my parents or grandparents with my first PR application?**
**Answer:** **No.** You’ll typically need to **sponsor** them later via the **Parents and Grandparents Program (PGP)** after meeting residency/income requirements.
