## Overview of the Canada Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)
The **Provincial Nominee Program** is second only to the **Express Entry program** in choice for aspirants seeking **Canada Permanent Residency**. This program allows **provinces** to **nominate** suitable candidates. Each **province** has its own **streams**, with at least one often connected to **Express Entry**. There are **more than 80 streams** active across Canada.  
Learn more: [**Canada Express Entry program**](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/skilled-migration-visa-consultant.html)

- **Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program**  
- **Northwest Territories Nominee Program**  
- **Yukon Nominee Program**  
- **Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program**  

Each province sets its own **immigration criteria**, and qualifying applicants receive a **nomination** that increases their **CRS score**, moving them closer to **Canada PR**.

## Skilled & Entrepreneur Streams within Canada PNP
Every **PNP** offers **skilled professional streams** and **entrepreneur/investor streams**. Applicants must **intend to settle** in the nominating province. Provinces prioritize **skilled professionals** based on local needs—for example, **BC** focuses on **tech**, **New Brunswick** on **healthcare**.  

Entrepreneurs must submit a **viable business plan** providing **local job opportunities**. Some provinces require a **12-month provisional period** on a **temporary visa** before issuing a nomination. This program has helped hundreds of migrants relocate to their chosen provinces.

## Why PNP Candidates Often Succeed in the Local Job Market
Research by **IRCC** shows that **PNP candidates** have higher chances of recruitment due to provincial selection aligning with local **skills**, **language capabilities**, **education**, and **experience**. The program supports **economic growth** across Canadian provinces.

## Applying via Express Entry and PNP (Together or Separately)
Applicants can apply through **Express Entry** and **PNP** simultaneously without conflict. Candidates in the **Express Entry pool** may still receive a **provincial nomination** if they meet provincial requirements.

## FAQs
**Which is better for Canada PR: PNP or Express Entry?**  
Both lead to **Canada PR**. **Express Entry** is federal and points-based; a **PNP nomination** adds **CRS points** and can fast-track selection.

**Can I apply to multiple provincial nominee programs at the same time?**  
Yes, you may **submit EOIs** to multiple provinces but should ultimately **accept one nomination**.

**Do all PNPs require a job offer?**  
Not always. Some streams require a **job offer**, others target **in-demand occupations**, **international graduates**, or **entrepreneurs**.

**Can I apply to a PNP if I’m already in the Express Entry pool?**  
Yes. Many **PNP streams** are aligned with **Express Entry**; a **provincial nomination** boosts your **CRS score** toward **Canada PR**.
