## Malta Residency by Investment Program

Through this program, **non-European investors** can obtain **European residency**. With this **VISA**, the investor can **travel across the European Union** with an **EU Residency Card**. To be eligible for this program, the investor has to **invest a minimum of Euro 250,000** in **Government bonds**. This investment should be maintained by the investor for a period of **5 years**. Along with this, the investor has to make a **non-refundable donation of Euro 30,000** to the Government fund and should **buy a real estate property of Euro 300,000** if investing in **North Malta** or **Comino**. If the purchase is made in **Gozo** or **South Malta**, then the property can be **Euro 270,000**. **Or** the investor has to take a **rental property** on lease at **Euro 12,000 per annum**.

With [**MRVP**](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.ae/malta-residency-visa-programme.html), investors can **live indefinitely in Malta**. This **residency** is valid for **5 years** and is **renewable**, although the investor needs to maintain the **investment only for 5 years**. Also, this program **doesn’t need** the investor to **live in Malta** at any given point in time. This **VISA** is **extendable to family members** as well. Once all the paperwork and process go through, the investor will be granted their **Maltese Residency certificate**, which will be **monitored every year for the first five years**. Post which, the certificate is **monitored every 5 years**.

## Malta Citizenship by Investment

**Malta’s Citizenship by Investment (CBI)** is also termed the [**Malta Individual Investor Program**](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/malta-citizenship-by-investment.html). This is one of the most unique programs in the world. Obtaining **Maltese citizenship** has many advantages to it. The **Maltese passport** allows **visa-free travel to 170+ countries**. It provides the citizens the right to **live, work, or study** in any part of the **European Union** and **Switzerland**. **Malta** has set a **standard** for the investors to comply with and that needs to be adhered to, to qualify for this program. The investor will have to make a **contribution of Euro 650,000**, along with purchasing **bonds worth Euro 150,000** along with **property purchase worth Euro 350,000**. If the investor **doesn’t want to purchase a property**, then he/she will be investing in a **rental property** with an annual lease of **Euro 12,000**. In addition to this, there will be a **Government process fee**. If the investor is applying **along with family**, then the **donation fee would be Euro 675,000**, with **no change** in **bond price** and **real estate investments**.

## FAQs

**Is the Malta Residency by Investment (MRVP) truly passive—do I need to live in Malta?**
The **residency** is valid even without continuous residence; investors **do not need to live in Malta** to maintain status (as per program terms in this article).

**What are the key financial components for Malta citizenship by investment?**
A **government contribution** (e.g., **Euro 650,000**), **bonds (Euro 150,000)**, and either a **property purchase (Euro 350,000)** or **rental (Euro 12,000 per year)**, plus **government fees**.

**Can my family be included under these Malta programs?**
Yes, both **residency** and **citizenship** routes allow **family inclusion**, subject to meeting program requirements and fees.

**What are the main benefits of Maltese status for investors?**
**EU mobility**, access to **world-class education and healthcare**, **tax advantages**, and potential **visa-free travel to 170+ countries** (for **citizenship**).
