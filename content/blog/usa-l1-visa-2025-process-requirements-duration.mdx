## What Is the USA L1 Visa?

The **USA L1 visa** is designed for employees of **multinational companies** who are relocating to the **U.S.** to work in a **managerial**, **executive**, or **specialized knowledge** role.

### L1 Categories

1. **L1A visa:** For **managers and executives**.
2. **L1B visa:** For employees with **specialized knowledge**.

This category also allows your **spouse and children** to accompany you on **L1** and **L2** status, with the added benefit that **L2 spouses** can **work in the U.S.** after obtaining an **Employment Authorization Document (EAD)**.

### USA Visa L1 Requirements

To qualify, you’ll need to meet certain **US visa L1 requirements**:

- You must have **worked** for the **parent, subsidiary, or affiliate** outside the **U.S.** for **at least one continuous year** in the **last three years**.
- The **U.S. entity** should be a **qualifying office** (either existing or **newly established**).
- For a **new office**, companies must show a **solid business plan**, **office space**, and **operational capacity**.

#### L1 Visa Duration of Stay in the US

How long can you stay on an **L1 visa**? It depends on your category:

- **New office setup:** **1 year** initially.
- **L1A visa:** Up to **7 years** total (initial **3 years** + extensions).
- **L1B visa:** Up to **5 years** total.

Since the **L1 visa duration of stay in the US** is limited, many applicants eventually transition to **permanent residency**, taking advantage of the L1’s **dual intent** flexibility (e.g., **EB-1C** for multinational managers/executives).

## L1 Visa Processing Time

The **L1 visa processing** time varies, but here’s a general idea:

- **Standard processing:** A few weeks to several months.
- **Premium processing:** Around **15 calendar days**.

The process involves filing **Form I-129** with **USCIS**, receiving the **I-797** approval notice, and completing **visa stamping** at a **U.S. consulate**.

For businesses needing urgent transfers, **premium processing** can significantly reduce delays.

## Entering US with L1 Visa

Once approved, **entering US with L1 visa status** is straightforward but requires preparation:

- Present your valid **passport**, **L1 visa**, and **approval documents** at the **port of entry**.
- **CBP** officers will grant you **L1 status** and issue/stamp your **I-94**.
- Remember, your **spouse (L2)** can **work** once they get an **EAD**.

## H1B to L1A Conversion in USA

Already in the **U.S.** on an **H1B**? Switching through an **H1B to L1A conversion** in the **USA** could be a game-changer.

- To qualify, you must have **worked for your employer abroad** for at least **1 of the last 3 years**.
- Unlike **H1B**, the **L1A** avoids **lottery** restrictions and allows for **dual intent**, making it easier to plan for **permanent residency**.

This pathway is popular for professionals seeking **managerial** roles and **long-term relocation** opportunities.

## FAQs

**Q1. What’s the difference between L1A and L1B?**
A. **L1A** is for **managers/executives** (max **7 years**); **L1B** is for **specialized knowledge** employees (max **5 years**). Both permit **dual intent** and allow **dependents** (L2).

**Q2. How fast can I get an L1 approved?**
A. **USCIS premium processing** can return action in about **15 calendar days**; otherwise **standard processing** can take **weeks to months**, plus **consular scheduling**.

**Q3. Can my L2 spouse work in the U.S.?**
A. Yes. **L2 spouses** are eligible to **work** after receiving **EAD**; they can also **study** while in the **U.S.**

**Q4. Does an L1 lead to a Green Card?**
A. L1 status allows **dual intent**. Many **L1A** holders pursue **EB-1C** (multinational manager/executive) for **permanent residency**, subject to eligibility and evidence.
