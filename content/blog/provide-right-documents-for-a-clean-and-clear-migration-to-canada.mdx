To **migrate to Canada**, one needs to understand the different pathways the country offers. **Canada** provides solutions for skilled professionals, students, and high-net-worth investors. Each stream has defined pathways.

As skilled professionals, applicants look for jobs that enhance their careers. **Canada**, being a large country, often requires foreign candidates. With **Canada's immigration policies**, migrants can obtain **Canada Permanent Residency (PR)** efficiently. Working with **ICCRC immigration consultants** ensures your application meets all requirements.

**Canada** introduced the **Express Entry System** to filter suitable candidates. The portal evaluates applications based on age, education, work experience, language ability, and adaptability. Each factor is scored, and top profiles are processed further.

## Documents Required for Canada Immigration

### 1. Documents for Age
- **Birth Certificate**
- **Aadhar Card** or any official identification proving your date of birth
*Maximum score for age: 12 points*

### 2. Documents for Education
- **Education Credential Assessment (ECA) Report** by a designated organization verifying equivalency with Canadian certificates, diplomas, or degrees
*Maximum score for education: 25 points*

### 3. Documents for Work Experience
- **Certificate of Experience** or **Experience Letter** from employers
- Full-time: 1 year with 30 hours/week
- Part-time: equivalent 24 months with 15 hours/week
- Experience counts if in **Canada**, abroad, or during studies in Canada, including self-employment
*Maximum score: 15 points*

### 4. Documents for Language Ability
- Mandatory approved language test for **English** (Canadian Language Benchmarks) or **French** (NCLC)
- Report must cover reading, writing, speaking, and listening
- Scores: 7 for **Express Entry**, minimum 5 for **Canada PNP programs**
*Maximum score: 15 points*

### 5. Documents for Adaptability
- Relevant spouse documents to gain additional points:
  - Spouse **ECA Report**
  - Spouse **Work Experience Certificates**
  - Spouse **Language Test Report**
*Maximum score: 10 points*

### 6. Other Important Documents
- **Job Offer** for applicant/spouse (optional but beneficial)
- Relatives in **Canada** (optional)
- **Bank Statement** proving financial stability without reliance on public funds

## FAQs

1. **What documents are mandatory for Canada Express Entry?**
A. **Birth certificate**, **ECA report**, **work experience certificates**, approved **language test report**, and financial documents are mandatory.

2. **Can my spouse's documents improve my Express Entry score?**
A. Yes, **spouse ECA**, **work experience**, and **language ability reports** contribute to adaptability points.

3. **Do I need a job offer to apply for Canada PR?**
A. A **job offer** is optional but increases your score. Other factors like relatives in **Canada** may also help.
