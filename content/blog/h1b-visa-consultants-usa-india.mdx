Highly skilled professionals in fields such as **engineering, architecture, arts, law, accounting, mathematics, sciences, medicine, education, and business** can apply for the **[H-1B visa to the USA](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/blog/H-1B-visa-consultants-for-the-USA-in-India.html)**.

The H-1B is a **non-immigrant visa with dual intent**, allowing you to work temporarily and later pursue a **Green Card**. Multiple employment is allowed with separate **I-129 petition approvals** from each employer.

## H-1B Visa Eligibility Requirements

- Hold a **U.S. bachelor’s or master’s degree**, or an equivalent qualification
- Have a **job offer** for a professional-level position
- Demonstrate relevant **knowledge and expertise**
- Possess **minimum 2 years of work experience**

## Duration of H-1B Visa

- Initial stay: **3 years**
- Extension: Additional **3 years**
- Maximum: Up to **10 years** for certain roles

## H-1B Visa Application Process

### 1. Find a U.S. Employer Sponsor
Secure a job offer from a U.S. company willing to sponsor your H-1B visa.

### 2. Submit Labor Condition Application (LCA)
The employer files an LCA with the **Department of Labor (DOL)**, confirming the job meets wage and labor requirements.

### 3. Submit Form I-129 Petition
The employer submits **Form I-129** with required documents:
- Education & experience certificates
- Training certificates
- Resume & job offer letter
- Professional memberships

### 4. Visa Stamping & Embassy Process
After I-129 approval, the applicant completes the process at the **U.S. Embassy or Consulate** in India.

## Benefits of H-1B Visa

- Work for **multiple U.S. employers** (with separate approvals)
- Dual intent visa (temporary to permanent residency)
- Multi-entry visa
- Gain new skills and professional growth
- Competitive income and benefits (health, disability, retirement)
- Improved lifestyle and access to **free primary education**

## FAQs

**Who is eligible for the H-1B visa?**
Professionals with a U.S. bachelor’s or master’s degree (or equivalent), a job offer for a specialty occupation, relevant experience, and expertise.

**How long can an H-1B visa holder stay in the USA?**
The initial stay is 3 years, extendable by 3 more years, with a maximum of 10 years for certain roles.

**Can I work for multiple U.S. employers on an H-1B visa?**
Yes, but each employer must submit a separate I-129 petition and get approval.

**What is the benefit of the H-1B visa being a dual-intent visa?**
It allows you to work temporarily in the USA while also applying for permanent residency (Green Card).
