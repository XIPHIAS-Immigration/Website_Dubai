**Malta** has long been a sought-after destination for global investors, retirees, and families looking for **European residency** without the need to relocate full-time. With the implementation of **Legal Notice 146 of 2025**, the country has introduced several **key reforms** to its **Malta Permanent Residence Program (MPRP)**, aimed at making it more efficient, transparent, and appealing, especially for families and **property investors**.

Let’s take a detailed look at the key updates to **Malta’s Permanent Residence Program**, and why they’re a game-changer for investors, families, and global residents alike.

## 1. Temporary One-Year Residence Permit: Start Living in Malta Sooner

One of the most significant updates is the introduction of a **temporary one-year residence permit**. Under the revised rules, applicants and their eligible **family members** can receive this temporary permit **as soon as they pass the initial due diligence stage**.

### Why is this important?
Previously, applicants had to wait several months for full approval before being able to **reside in Malta**. Now, with this permit, they can **legally live in Malta almost immediately** after preliminary approval. This is especially beneficial for **families with children** who want to enroll in schools, or retirees looking to start enjoying **Malta’s lifestyle** without delay.

If the application is ultimately rejected, the permit is **revoked within 15 days**, ensuring regulatory integrity.

## 2. New Unified Fee Structure: Simpler and More Transparent Costs

The **MPRP** has moved to a **clear and unified fee system**, making it easier for applicants to plan financially. Here's how it breaks down:

- **Administrative Fee:** **€60,000**, paid in two parts:
  - **€15,000** upon submission of the application
  - **€45,000** after issuance of a **Letter of Approval in Principle**
- **Government Contribution:** A **flat €37,000**, regardless of whether the applicant chooses to **buy or lease** a property.
- **Donation Requirement:** **€2,000** donation to a registered **Maltese NGO**.

> **Note:** The updated fees level the playing field, making the **leasing route** just as financially viable as **property ownership**.

## 3. Family-Friendly Reforms: Better Terms for Dependents

The updated program now allows more **inclusive and affordable** options for **dependents**, with clarified rules that help families plan more effectively:

- **Spouse** and **children under 18**: covered under the main applicant’s contribution (**no extra charge**).
- **Adult children (18+)**: **€7,500** each.
- **Disabled adult children**: **Free of charge**.
- **Parents and grandparents**: Still eligible, provided they are **not in full-time employment**.

These changes make it easier for **multi-generational families** to apply together while **reducing the financial burden** for larger families.

## 4. New Leasing Rights: Earn Income from Your Investment

For applicants who choose to **purchase a qualifying property** in **Malta**, the programme now allows the option to **lease the property out** if they are not residing in the country.

**Why does this matter?** Previously, strict rules prohibited renting out the **investment property**. Now, you can generate **rental income**, helping to **offset your investment cost**—ideal for those maintaining **EU residency** for travel and business but not living in **Malta** full-time.

## 5. Licensing for Agents: Ensuring Trust and Accountability

Going forward, anyone assisting applicants—**agents**, **consultants**, or **legal advisors**—must hold a **valid annual license** from the responsible **Maltese authority** and pass thorough **due diligence** checks.

**What this means for you:** You’ll work with **verified, qualified professionals** who meet high standards of compliance, ensuring a **smoother, safer** application experience.

## Why These 2025 Changes Matter

These updates make **Malta’s MPRP** more than just a **residency-by-investment** scheme—they make it **a practical and accessible pathway** for individuals and families seeking:

- **Early access to EU residency**
- **Simplified and transparent costs**
- **Rental income potential** from **property**
- **Inclusive family relocation** options
- **Peace of mind** through **licensed, regulated advisors**

**Malta** maintains a careful balance between welcoming **international residents** and upholding strong **due diligence** and **legal integrity**.

## FAQs

**Do the 2025 rules let me live in Malta while my MPRP is processed?**
Yes. After you pass **initial due diligence**, a **temporary one-year residence permit** lets you **reside in Malta** while your application continues.

**Is leasing my qualifying property allowed under MPRP 2025?**
Under the updates, **owners of qualifying property** may **lease it out** when not residing, enabling potential **rental income**.

**How much should I budget for government fees and contributions?**
Plan for **€60,000 administrative fees** (split **€15k + €45k**), a **€37,000 government contribution**, and a **€2,000 NGO donation**—plus your **property** route (buy or lease).

**Can I include extended family members?**
Yes. **Spouse** and **children under 18** are included at **no extra cost**, **adult children** at **€7,500** each, **disabled adult children free**, and **parents/grandparents** if **not in full-time employment**.
