The **United States of America (USA)**, consisting of **50 states**, a **federal district**, and **five self-administering nations**, is a global superpower and a top destination for foreign investors. One key program is the **US EB-5 Permanent Residency Program**, which allows investors to secure **Green Cards** while contributing to the U.S. economy.

## What is the US EB-5 Permanent Residency Program?

The **EB-5 Visa (Employment-Based Fifth Preference)** is an **immigration program** introduced in the 1990s to:

- Create **job opportunities for U.S. workers**
- Attract **foreign investment** to stimulate the **U.S. economy**

Participants in the program gain several benefits, including the opportunity to **acquire an EB-5 Green Card**.

### What is a Regional Center?

An **EB-5 Regional Center** is an entity designated by **USCIS (U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services)** to work with **investors** and **enterprises** under the EB-5 program. Regional Centers facilitate **economic development**, allowing investors to participate **indirectly** in projects while still qualifying for a Green Card.

## Requirements for Investing in a Regional Center

- **Minimum Investment**: $500,000 USD
- **Administration Fee**: Approximately $50,000 USD (varies by Regional Center)
- Recommended to consult a **financial expert** before investing
- The investor is **not required** to manage day-to-day operations

## Advantages of Choosing a Regional Center Investment

- Investors can **live anywhere in the U.S.**, regardless of project location
- **Job creation responsibility** lies with the Regional Center, not the investor
- Investors can continue their **own professional or business activities**
- No need to **manage daily operations** of the investment project

Once the **Investor’s petition** is approved by **USCIS**, the investor and their family receive a **conditional Green Card** valid for **two years**.

**NOTE:** Apply **three months before expiration** of the conditional Green Card to **remove conditions**. Upon approval, **USCIS** will issue a **permanent Green Card**.

## FAQs

**Q1. Difference between Regional Center EB-5 and Direct EB-5?**
A. Regional Center EB-5 counts **indirect jobs** and is managed by third parties, offering a **passive investment**. Direct EB-5 requires creating **10 full-time W-2 jobs** in the investor’s enterprise, giving more **control** but requiring **active management**.

**Q2. What happens after receiving a conditional Green Card?**
A. You receive a **two-year conditional Green Card**. File to **remove conditions** within the **90-day window before expiration**, providing proof of **job creation** and continued **investment** to obtain permanent residency.

**Q3. Can I live anywhere in the U.S. if I invest via a Regional Center?**
A. Yes. Investors may **reside anywhere in the U.S.**, even if the EB-5 project is located in a different **state** or **Targeted Employment Area (TEA)**.
