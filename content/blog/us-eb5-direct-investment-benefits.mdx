## US EB-5 Visa Overview

The **United States** has always been a popular destination for **businessmen** and **professionals** seeking the American dream by establishing a company. The **Immigrant Investor Program**, also known as the **EB-5 Visa**, provides a pathway for **investors and their immediate families** to obtain a **Green Card** and become **lawful permanent residents** of the U.S.

The first critical choice for investors is whether to invest **directly** or through an **EB-5 Regional Center**. Understanding the **differences** between these pathways is key to achieving your **immigration and business goals**.
*Learn more:* **EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program**

## EB-5 Immigrant Investor Visa Pathways

- **Direct EB-5 Investment**: Requires an investment in a **real operating business** that results in **10 full-time jobs**. I-526 approval for direct investment is often **faster** than through a Regional Center. Investors can **withdraw funds** after business obligations are met.
- **Regional Center EB-5**: Investments are pooled, and job creation can be **direct or indirect**. Typically involves a **5–9 year lock-in period**, with less control for the investor.

In a **Direct Investment**, the investor can either **develop a venture** based on an innovative idea or invest in an **existing operating business**, taking responsibility for **creating and maintaining jobs** for **qualified U.S. workers** (excluding family members).

## Top Benefits of an EB-5 Direct Investment

- **Shorter Timelines to Start Business** – Unlike Regional Center projects that take **9–12 months** for pre-approvals and compliance, **Direct EB-5 investments** allow immediate **business development, marketing, and external funding**.
- **Lower Administrative Fees** – Direct investment minimizes **overhead costs**, allowing full deployment of capital toward the business, unlike Regional Center fees, which are **in addition** to the investment amount.
- **Higher Control on Business** – Investors directly **manage and operate** the business, unlike Regional Centers where control is limited.
- **Potential for Higher ROI** – Direct EB-5 offers **uncapped returns**, similar to entrepreneurial ventures, whereas Regional Center investments tend to generate **lower returns** due to risk pooling.

### Key Differences: EB-5 Direct Investment vs. Regional Centers

| **Advantages** | **Direct EB-5 Investment** | **Regional Center EB-5 Investment** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Type of Investment** | Direct investment | Indirect investment |
| **Source of Funds** | Investor provides proof of funds | Investor provides proof of funds |
| **Job Creation & No. of Jobs** | 10 direct jobs | Direct/Indirect jobs per Regional Center model |
| **Ownership** | Investor owns equity | Investor gives a loan for ~5 years with exit option |
| **Management** | Investor manages company | Regional Center appoints management company |
| **Control** | Full control | Minimal control; investor is one of many |
| **Experience Required** | Must have industry experience | No experience required |
| **Timelines** | 12–18 months | 18–24 months |
| **Return on Investment** | Higher potential | Lower returns, less risk |

## Conclusion

Prospective **EB-5 investors** can choose **Direct Investment** or **Regional Center** routes. The choice depends on your **investment goals, control preference, and risk tolerance**. Direct EB-5 is ideal for investors seeking **greater control** and **higher ROI**, while Regional Centers offer **simplified administration** with **less personal involvement**.

## FAQs

**Q1. How much do I need to invest for EB-5 and what counts toward the minimum?**
A. Minimum investment depends on **TEA designation**. Only **capital investment and job creation funds** count; **administrative/legal fees** are excluded.

**Q2. For Direct EB-5, how are the 10 jobs proven to USCIS?**
A. Investors must show **10 full-time W-2 jobs** for **qualified U.S. workers** using **payroll records, I-9s, and organizational charts** during I-829 processing.

**Q3. Is a Regional Center safer than Direct EB-5?**
A. Regional Centers **diversify risk** and allow indirect job creation, but typically provide **lower ROI** and less control. Direct EB-5 provides **full management control** and potentially **higher returns**, but requires job creation responsibility.
