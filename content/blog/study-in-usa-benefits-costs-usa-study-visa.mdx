## Overview: Why the United States Attracts International Students

**United States of America** is always the top choice for all those intending to **migrate**. One of the reasons is the **vibrance** of the country, which allows migrants to explore avenues to grow and have a better life, while contributing significantly to the country. The **Indian community in USA** can be attributed to the financial growth witnessed by the country. **2.7 million Indian immigrants** have made the USA their home as per data up till **2019**.

## Costs of Studying in USA

As said earlier, if not planned well, the **cost of education in the USA** can put a hole in the pockets. **Study in USA** cost could range **anywhere between USD $10,000 to USD $100,000 annually**. To address this kind of **fee structure**, one needs to explore **scholarships**, **tuition waivers**, **grants**, **research** and **teaching assistantships**, and more. These aids can be provided by **private organizations**, **Government bodies**, or **educational institutes**. Again, since the competition for **studying in USA** is high, there will be high competition for these aids too. Hence, it would require the student to **prepare in advance** and conduct **thorough research** or take **guidance from experts**.

## FAQs

**1) What is the basic USA study visa pathway for international students?**
**Answer:** Most students apply for the **F-1 Student Visa** (for full-time academic study). **J-1** is for **exchange/short-term** programs. Your school’s **I-20/DS-2019**, **SEVIS fee**, and a **consular interview** are core steps. See **USA Study Visa**.

**2) How can I reduce my cost to study in USA?**
**Answer:** Apply early for **scholarships**, **tuition waivers**, **research/teaching assistantships**, and explore **in-state rates** (where applicable) or **payment plans**. Strong **applications** and **test scores** help.
