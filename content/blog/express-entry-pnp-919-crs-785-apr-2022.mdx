## Express Entry PNP Draw — April 1, 2022

**Canada** invited **919** immigrants to apply for **Permanent Residence (PR)** under the **Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)** via the **Express Entry** system.
The minimum **CRS (Comprehensive Ranking System)** score was **785**, which combines a **base CRS** plus **+600** points awarded for a **provincial nomination**. Candidates who receive an invitation typically have **60 days** to submit a complete **eAPR**.

---

## What is the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)?

Launched in **1998**, the **PNP** lets **provinces/territories** nominate candidates who match local labour-market needs for **PR**. While each province sets its own criteria, common factors include **language proficiency**, **education**, **work experience/training**, and **proof of funds**. A **PNP nomination** adds **+600 CRS** points in **Express Entry**, greatly improving the chance of receiving an **ITA**.

---

## Popular PNP Pathways (Overview)

### OINP — Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program
**Ontario** invites candidates across several streams. After a notification of interest, applicants generally have **45 days** to submit documents. A successful **OINP** nomination adds **+600 CRS** and can lead to an **ITA**.

### AINP — Alberta Advantage Immigration Program (AAIP)
**Alberta** nominates candidates through streams such as the **Alberta Opportunity Stream**, **Express Entry-aligned** selections (notification of interest), and a **Self-Employed Farmer** stream.

### MPNP — Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program
Key routes include **Skilled Workers Overseas**, **Express Entry Pathway**, and **Human Capital Pathway**. Points consider **age**, **education**, **experience**, **language**, and **adaptability** (e.g., family/friend ties in **Manitoba**).

### BC PNP — British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program
Streams include **Skilled Immigration**, **Express Entry BC**, and **Entrepreneur Immigration**, targeting priority sectors such as **IT** and **healthcare** and supporting faster processing aligned with **Express Entry**.

### SINP — Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program
Under **SINP**, international skilled workers typically require **post-secondary credentials** (often with **ECA**), eligible occupations, and an **EOI** profile; some streams link to **Express Entry**.

## What to Do If You’re Invited

- Submit your **eAPR** within the IRCC deadline (**60 days**).
- Include **identity**, **education (ECA)**, **language** results, **work** documents, **police** certificates, **medical** exams, and **proof of funds** where applicable.

## FAQs

**1) Why was the cutoff high (CRS 785)?**
**PNP-only** draws include nominees with **+600 CRS** points, which raises the cutoff compared with all-program draws.

**2) Do I need a job offer to get nominated?**
Not always. Some **PNP** streams require a **job offer**; others focus on **skills**, **occupation demand**, or **ties** to the province.

**3) How long from nomination to PR?**
Timelines vary by province and **IRCC** processing, often **~15–19 months** after nomination.

**4) Does a nomination guarantee an ITA?**
A nomination adds **+600 CRS**, which almost always leads to an **ITA** in **PNP-only** or **all-program** draws, subject to **IRCC** rounds and cutoffs.
