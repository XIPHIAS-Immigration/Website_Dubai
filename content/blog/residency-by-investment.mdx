Economic convergence has been a catalyst for diminishing border lines. Despite the challenges posed by **Covid-19**, developing countries have been taking resolute steps to augment their **visa-free travel regimes**. The **European Union’s Schengen Area** initiative is a great example of uniting EU markets by facilitating **free mobility in Schengen countries**.

## Global Mobility Fosters Economic Development

Migration is a global phenomenon that has been one of the key factors in shaping economies and societies for ages. Migration contributes significantly to global prosperity — though migrants constitute only 3% of the global population, they yield nearly 9% of the **global GDP**.

With changes in the global landscape, many countries are actively working to attract new skills, business investments, innovative ideas, and **real estate investments** by relaxing **visa regulations** and enabling **visa-free travel**. Such policies encourage **tourism, international trade**, and **employment of skilled workers**. Globally, tourism accounts for about 6.5% of total business, but in some countries, tourism contributes more than 20% of their economy. Other major contributors include **Foreign Direct Investment (FDI)** and the inflow of foreign skilled workers.

## Growth of Residency and Citizenship by Investment Programs

The **pandemic** has transformed global mobility patterns, prompting countries to compete for **foreign investments** through **investment migration programs**. These programs have become popular among **high-net-worth individuals (HNIs)** seeking to acquire a **second citizenship** or **residency** as a secure long-term investment.

According to a global survey by CS Global Partners, **89%** of respondents expressed interest in holding a **second citizenship**, and nearly **80%** were willing to invest up to **5% of their annual income** to obtain it.

Global citizens are increasingly seeking stability, **freedom of movement**, better **healthcare**, **education**, and **social security** by investing in countries offering **residency by investment** programs. This demand has also created a need for **certified investment migration consultants** who can guide HNIs and businesses toward informed global investment and migration decisions.

## Returns on Investment (RoI) Through Residency Programs

The pandemic increased the demand for capital worldwide, pushing governments to attract **FDI, new talent, and skills** through appealing **residency and citizenship programs**.

There is a growing need for expert advice on investing for **residency or second citizenship**. Popular options include:
- **Investing in an existing or new business**
- **Real estate investment**
- **Investment in government-approved funds**

### Residency by Business Investment

Migration through **business investment** has been a long-established practice in countries like the **USA, UK, and Australia**. The **USA’s EB-5 Visa Program** saw a 400% increase in applications before the pandemic.

There are two key categories of **business investment residency**:

- **Investing in a New Business:** Ideal for investors ready to expand globally and generate employment. Countries like the **USA, UK, Canada, Australia**, and **New Zealand** are leading destinations for business-based residency.

- **Starting an Innovative Business:** Many nations have introduced **Startup Visa** or **Innovator Visa Programs** to attract entrepreneurial talent. Popular programs include the **Canada Start-up Visa** and the **UK Innovator Visa**.

### Residency by Real Estate Investment

Many developed and developing economies offer **residency or citizenship** in exchange for **real estate investment**. This option provides promising returns, **freedom to reside**, and **visa-free travel** to multiple destinations.

Top countries offering such programs include **Portugal, Turkey, Spain, Greece, Malta**, and several **Caribbean countries** like **Dominica, St. Lucia, St. Kitts and Nevis, Grenada**, and **Antigua & Barbuda**.

### Residency by Fund Investment

Over recent years, **fund investments** have matured into an attractive route for acquiring **residency**, particularly in **European countries**. This is favored by investors who prefer passive investment. The **Singapore Global Investor Program** and various European **investment funds** in **Portugal, Spain, Greece**, and **Italy** are among the most popular.
