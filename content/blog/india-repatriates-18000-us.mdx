In a significant diplomatic development, **India** has expressed its readiness to collaborate with the **United States** to **repatriate approximately 18,000 Indian citizens** currently residing illegally in the U.S. This initiative is part of India's broader strategy to maintain and strengthen its **bilateral relationship** with the U.S., particularly regarding **immigration** and **trade policies**.

## Background and Context

The **U.S.** has identified around 18,000 **Indian nationals** living without legal authorization. Some estimates suggest the actual number of **undocumented Indian immigrants** could be higher. For instance, a 2022 report by the **U.S. Department of Homeland Security** estimated around 220,000 unauthorized Indian immigrants were residing in the U.S. at that time.

Historically, **India** has cooperated with the **U.S.** on repatriation. This latest move aligns with the U.S. administration's focus on curbing **illegal immigration** and ensures that **legal immigration pathways** remain open and robust.

## Protecting Legal Immigration Channels

A key motivation for **India** is to safeguard **legal immigration channels**, which are essential for its citizens. The [**H-1B visa program**](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/blog/Work-Prohibition-on-Spouses-of-US-H1B-Visa-Holders-has-Reprieved.html) is critical for **skilled Indian professionals** seeking U.S. employment. In 2023, **Indian citizens** accounted for nearly **75% of the 386,000 H-1B visas granted**.

Similarly, **student visas** are vital for Indians pursuing higher education in the **U.S.** By addressing **illegal immigration**, India ensures these **legal migration avenues** remain accessible and potentially expanded in the future.

## Strategic Considerations

India’s proactive approach also serves broader **strategic objectives**:

- Avoiding potential **trade conflicts** with the U.S., especially regarding tariffs
- Aligning with domestic priorities, such as curbing overseas support for secessionist movements like the **Khalistan movement**
- Strengthening **bilateral relations** while maintaining open **legal migration pathways**

## Recent Developments

In October 2024, the **U.S. Department of Homeland Security** chartered a repatriation flight returning over 100 **Indian nationals** to India. This built upon more than **1,100 deportations** in the preceding year, highlighting collaborative efforts to manage **illegal migration**.

## Official Statements

**Randhir Jaiswal**, spokesperson for India’s Ministry of External Affairs, stated:
*"As part of India-U.S. cooperation on migration and mobility, both sides are engaged in a process to deter illegal migration. This is being done to create more avenues for legal migration from India to the U.S."*

## Conclusion

India’s initiative to **repatriate 18,000 citizens** underscores its commitment to addressing **illegal immigration** while safeguarding **essential legal migration channels**. By cooperating with the **U.S.**, India strengthens **bilateral ties**, protects **economic interests**, and ensures opportunities for its citizens in the **U.S.** remain robust.

India’s cooperation aims to **safeguard legal channels** like **H-1B visas** and **student visas** for skilled professionals and students.

Yes, India has a history of working with the **U.S.** on repatriating citizens and addressing **illegal migration**.
