**Nursing or healthcare careers** are in high demand in **New Zealand**. The **Government of New Zealand** has made it convenient for foreigners to get **visas** to work and settle permanently. To work as a **nurse** or other **healthcare professional**, you must meet **nursing registration requirements**, complete the **Competence Assessment Programme (CAP)**, and fulfill CAP fund obligations. Specific **work visas** are required to pursue these opportunities.

## Apply for the Following Visa

- **Occupational Registration Visitor Visa**
- **Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV)**

## Before Submitting a Visa Application

- You must be eligible to work as a **nurse** in **New Zealand**.
- Be registered with the **Nursing Council of New Zealand (NCNZ)**.
- Successfully complete the **Competence Assessment Programme (CAP)**.
- Ensure eligibility for a visa through **Immigration New Zealand (INZ)**.

## FAQs

**Who can apply for nursing jobs in New Zealand?**
Registered nurses and healthcare professionals who meet NCNZ requirements and visa eligibility can apply.

**Which visas are available for nurses in New Zealand?**
The **Occupational Registration Visitor Visa** and **Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV)** are the primary options.

**Do I need to complete the Competence Assessment Programme (CAP)?**
Yes, completing CAP is mandatory to work as a registered nurse in New Zealand.

**Can I settle permanently in New Zealand as a nurse?**
Yes, eligible nurses can apply for long-term residency after meeting work, registration, and visa requirements.
