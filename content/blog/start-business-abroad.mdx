## Investment for the Purpose of Conducting Business

Many **entrepreneurs** intend to **start their business** in a new country to capitalize on growth potential. Examples include **Mr. Amar Bose**, who relocated to **USA** from **India** and founded the **Bose Corporation**, and **farmers from Punjab** who moved to **California** to build the **almond farming** industry. These illustrate successful ventures started from scratch in a new country. The following programs encourage **innovative thinkers** to start businesses abroad:

### UK Start-up Program

The **UK Start-up Visa** is for individuals with a **business idea** they want to launch in the **UK**. The ideas should be **innovative**, **viable**, and **scalable**, with potential for international growth. Entrepreneurs must provide an **endorsement** from an **approved endorsement body** and complete required clearances.

Successful applicants receive a **2-year visa**, non-renewable, after which they can transition to the **UK Innovator Visa**, which is extendable. Eligibility for **permanent residency** follows **5 years** of living in the UK.

### Canada Start-up Program

The **Canada Start-up Visa** works similarly to the UK program. Applicants must submit a strong business idea supported by a **Letter of Support** from a **designated organization** approved by **Canada Immigration**. Up to **5 applicants** may jointly apply, each providing a **Letter of Support**. Successful applicants gain **Canada permanent residency** and eventual **citizenship**.

### France Talent Passport Program

Entrepreneurs relocating to **France** may apply under the **France Talent Passport – Skilled Residence Permit (Business Creator)**. The visa must be stamped within **2 months** of arrival. Businesses can be **commercial**, **artisanal**, or **industrial**, subject to **French regulations**. The visa is valid for **4 years**, after which the entrepreneur may apply for **permanent residency**.

### Start-up Ireland Visa Program

**Ireland** offers excellent infrastructure for entrepreneurs. The **Start-up Ireland Visa Program** supports business creation with **global reach**, attracting multi-nationals due to **low corporate tax** and infrastructure. **XIPHIAS Immigration** provides full setup support for entrepreneurs investing in Ireland.

### Canada Investment Immigration Program

This program invites experienced business investors to **start a business in Canada**, either by buying an existing business entirely or partially. Investments can be made in various **provinces**. The program provides **permanent residency** after meeting all **investment regulations**. There are two streams: **Entrepreneur** and **Farm Investment**.

## FAQs

**1) Which investment immigration program is best to start a business abroad?**
Depends on your **country preference**, **sector**, and **investment size**. Options include **UK Start-up/Innovator**, **Canada Start-Up Visa/Investment Immigration**, **France Talent Passport**, and **Ireland STEP**.

**2) Do I need to invest in real estate, or can I invest in a business?**
Most programs allow **business investment** (buying or creating a company). Some offer **real estate** routes, but for **business**, focus on **entrepreneur or investor streams**.

**3) Can multiple founders apply together for a start-up route?**
Yes. The **Canada Start-Up Visa** allows **up to 5 applicants** sharing a **Letter of Support** from a **designated organization**.

**4) How soon can investment immigration lead to permanent residency?**
Timelines vary by **program** and **country**. Many routes (e.g., **UK Innovator**, **Canada** entrepreneur streams, **France Talent Passport**) can lead to **permanent residency** after meeting **residency** and **business performance** criteria.
