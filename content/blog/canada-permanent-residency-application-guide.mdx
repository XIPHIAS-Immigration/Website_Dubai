## Canada PR Overview
Canada has an incredible and stable economy which is increasing the immigrant count every year. Permanent residency is the most trending and successful status which enables the holder with numerous benefits. This has raised the interest of many skilled candidates to apply for **Canada Permanent Residency (PR)**. However, some qualified candidates’ visas get rejected due to mistakes or improper application submission.

## Immigration Programs to Obtain Canada PR
There are many [immigration programs](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/canada-skilled-immigration-consultants.html) to obtain Canada PR. The **Express Entry system** is the fastest way to get Canada PR. This is an electronic point-based system.

### List of Immigration Programs
- **Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP)**
- **Federal Skilled Trade Program (FSTP)**
- **Canadian Experience Class (CEC)**
- **Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP)**
- **Quebec Skilled Worker Program (QSWP)**
- **Family Sponsorship Program**

## Are the Programs Similar?
Each immigration program is unique with different criteria. Among all stages of immigration, **documentation is the most critical**. Mistakes here can cause delays or rejection.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. Signatures that do not match the appended proof documents.
2. Incorrect fee payment for the selected program.
3. Uploading photos with incorrect dimensions.
4. Missing supporting documents for your immigration method. Late submissions cause processing issues.
5. Submitting hand-written applications instead of digital forms.
6. Applications submitted without a valid cover letter.

Following the immigration process under the guidance of experts greatly reduces the chance of rejection.

## FAQs
**1) Why is the PNP considered a fast route to PR?**  
A **provincial nomination** can add **+600 CRS** points in **Express Entry**, quickly leading to an **ITA** and speeding up the **PR** process.

**2) Do I need a job offer for PNP?**  
Not always. Some streams require a **job offer**, while others focus on **in-demand occupations**, **ties**, or human-capital factors.

**3) Do I have to live in the nominating province?**  
You should have a **genuine intent to reside** in the province that nominated you, especially during the initial settlement period.

**4) Can my family be included?**  
Yes. **Spouse/partner** and **dependent children** are typically included in PNP-based **PR** applications.
