Australia remains one of the most structured destinations for skilled migration, with clear permanent and provisional pathways for eligible applicants. In practice, “Australia PR” is not a single visa category. It is a process that may involve a points-tested route, a state-nominated pathway, a regional route, or an employer-sponsored option, depending on the applicant’s profile and the visa subclass involved. The Australian Government’s skilled migration framework continues to centre on SkillSelect, invitation-based applications, employer nomination routes, and regional pathways to permanent residence.

## What Is Australia PR?

Australia PR refers to permanent residence in Australia through an eligible immigration pathway. Depending on the visa granted, permanent residents may be able to live and work in Australia on an ongoing basis and, where eligible, later progress toward citizenship under Australian law. Some routes grant permanent residence directly, while others first place the applicant on a provisional or regional pathway that can later lead to permanent residence.

## Australia PR Pathways Explained

Australia PR can be approached through several skilled migration routes, and the right option depends on whether the application is points-tested, nomination-based, regional, or employer-sponsored.

### Skilled Independent Pathway

The Skilled Independent visa, Subclass 189, is a points-tested permanent visa for applicants who meet the requirements of the relevant stream, hold a suitable skills assessment, have an occupation on the relevant skilled occupation list, and receive an invitation to apply. It is designed for applicants who do not rely on employer or state sponsorship.

Learn more about this route on the [Subclass 189 page](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/skilled/australia/skilled-independent-189)

### State Nominated Pathway

The Skilled Nominated visa, Subclass 190, is another permanent route, but it involves nomination by an Australian state or territory. It sits within the broader SkillSelect framework and is commonly considered by applicants whose profiles align with state-level criteria. Because nomination requirements vary by jurisdiction, this route requires more than a general understanding of federal eligibility.

You can explore this option further on the [Subclass 190 page](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/skilled/australia/skilled-nominated-190)

### Regional Skilled Pathway

The Skilled Work Regional visa, Subclass 491, is a provisional regional visa for eligible applicants who are nominated by a state or territory or sponsored by an eligible family member in designated regional Australia.

### Employer-Sponsored Permanent Residence

Some applicants qualify through employer sponsorship rather than the points-tested invitation route. The Employer Nomination Scheme, Subclass 186, is a permanent visa that allows skilled workers nominated by an employer to live and work in Australia permanently. This route is distinct from the SkillSelect invitation pathway and is often relevant where a genuine long-term employer nomination is involved.

Read more on the [Subclass 186 page](https://www.xiphiasimmigration.com/skilled/australia/employer-nomination-scheme-186)

## Step-by-Step Australia PR Process

### 1. Identify the Right Pathway

The first stage is not submission. It is a strategy. Before preparing an application, the applicant needs to identify whether the most suitable route is independent, state-nominated, regional, or employer-sponsored. This decision affects the documentation approach, the sequence of steps, and whether the application goes through SkillSelect or through an employer nomination structure. Australia’s permanent work visa system clearly distinguishes sponsorship-needed and nomination-needed pathways from points-tested independent routes.

### 2. Check Basic Eligibility

Once the likely route is identified, the next step is to assess whether the applicant appears to meet the broad visa requirements. Depending on the pathway, this may involve occupation eligibility, English language evidence, work experience, qualification relevance, nomination or sponsorship requirements, and general visa criteria such as health and character. For the points-tested Subclass 189 stream, the Department specifically requires a relevant occupation, a suitable skills assessment, an invitation to apply, and satisfaction of the points test.

### 3. Obtain a Skills Assessment Where Required

A suitable skills assessment is a central part of many skilled migration applications. The Department states that obtaining a suitable skills assessment is mandatory for some visa subclasses and streams, and that the assessment must come from the relevant assessing authority for the occupation. The assessment process itself is handled by the designated authority, not by the visa applicant directly through the visa system.

### 4. Meet the English Language Requirement

English language evidence is another important stage in the Australian PR process. The Department requires evidence of English language proficiency for some Australian visas, and applicants using SkillSelect may need to include English test results in the EOI. The Department also notes that the approved English tests for visa purposes changed on 7 August 2025, so current test validity and accepted formats should always be checked against the latest official requirements before submission.

### 5. Submit an Expression of Interest Through SkillSelect

For points-tested skilled pathways, the next formal stage is usually the Expression of Interest, or EOI. SkillSelect is the Australian Government’s online system for skilled workers who want to express interest in applying for a visa to live and work in Australia. The Department states that an EOI must be submitted before an applicant can be invited to apply for the Skilled Independent, Skilled Nominated, or Skilled Work Regional visa. Submitting an EOI is not the same as lodging a visa application; it is an expression of interest that allows the government to consider the applicant for invitation rounds or for state and territory review where relevant.

### 6. Seek State Nomination or Sponsorship Where Applicable

Some applicants need a state or territory nomination, while others require employer sponsorship. The Department’s permanent work visa guidance distinguishes between sponsorship-needed and nomination-needed pathways, and invitation-round guidance notes that state and territory government nominations are separate from the Department’s own periodic invitation rounds.

### 7. Wait for Invitation to Apply

For SkillSelect pathways, the Department runs invitation rounds periodically during the program year. It also makes clear that invitation numbers may vary and that state or territory nominations are not affected by the Department’s invitation rounds in the same way. This is an important distinction because eligibility alone does not automatically result in an invitation.

### 8. Lodge the Visa Application

Once invited, the applicant moves from interest to a formal visa application. The Department states that if an applicant is invited, the visa application must be completed online and supporting documents uploaded within the permitted time window. For employer-sponsored permanent residence, the application proceeds under the employer nomination structure rather than the SkillSelect invitation system.

### 9. Complete Health, Character, and Final Processing Stages

Australia’s immigration process also involves standard visa requirements, such as health and character assessment, where applicable. These checks form part of the final review before a decision is made. The overall skilled migration framework is administered through the permanent Migration Program and related skilled visa processing priorities.

## Who Can Apply for an Australia PR?

Australia PR is generally relevant to skilled professionals and other eligible applicants whose profiles align with a recognised migration pathway. Depending on the route, this may include applicants with an eligible occupation, applicants able to secure employer nomination, applicants suited to regional pathways, or those whose profiles align with state nomination criteria. The skilled migration program is intended to attract migrants who contribute to the Australian economy and help fill skilled roles, while permanent work visa categories include independent, nominated, and employer-sponsored options.
