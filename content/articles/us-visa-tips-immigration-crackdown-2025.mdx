## US visa tips for international students and foreign workers amid immigration crackdown in the US

Immigration raids in the United States have raised significant concerns among individuals seeking to enter or remain in the country.

**By Varun Singh**  
**February 23, 2025 | 13:11 IST**

In recent months, immigration raids in the United States have sparked widespread concern among individuals seeking to enter or remain in the country. These raids, often carried out by Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE), have led to heightened scrutiny of visa applications, increased deportations, and growing anxiety within immigrant communities. For Indian applicants looking to travel, work, or settle in the U.S., these developments bring forth critical considerations that must be addressed.

---

## Understanding the Recent Immigration Raids

The recent immigration enforcement efforts have primarily targeted individuals who are undocumented or have violated the terms of their visas. Authorities have intensified workplace inspections, detentions, and deportations, signaling a stricter stance on immigration. The raids are part of broader policies aimed at curbing illegal immigration and ensuring that all foreign nationals in the U.S. comply with immigration laws.

---

## Recent Deportation Flights and Numbers

In recent developments, the U.S. has intensified its immigration enforcement efforts, leading to a significant number of deportation flights. Notably, on February 5, 2025, a U.S. military aircraft transported **104 Indian nationals** back to Amritsar, Punjab. This marked one of the longest deportation flights, during which deportees were reportedly handcuffed and had their legs chained.

Additionally, on February 14, 2025, the U.S. deported **119 migrants** from various countries—including China, Uzbekistan, Pakistan, and Afghanistan—to Panama. These individuals were temporarily housed in a shelter in the Darien region before plans for repatriation to their home countries were initiated.

These actions underscore the current administration’s commitment to stringent immigration policies, resulting in widespread deportations and international collaborations to manage the return of undocumented individuals.

---

## Why Indian Applicants Should Pay Attention

Although these raids primarily affect undocumented immigrants, their implications extend to legal visa applicants as well. Indian nationals applying for student visas (F-1), work visas (H-1B, L-1), or permanent residency (green card) should take note of the increased scrutiny on immigration documentation.

Any minor errors or misrepresentations in applications can now lead to denials or delays. Additionally, those already in the U.S. on temporary visas must ensure they remain compliant with visa conditions to avoid legal complications.

---

## Impact on Different Visa Categories

### Student Visas (F-1, J-1, M-1)

Indian students planning to study in the U.S. must be extra cautious about maintaining their visa status. Authorities are closely monitoring whether students are attending their designated institutions and maintaining full-time enrollment. Any deviation from visa terms—such as taking unauthorized employment or failing to attend classes—can lead to visa revocation and deportation.

**To stay compliant:**  
Enroll in a legitimate, accredited institution. Keep track of attendance and academic progress. Avoid unauthorized work and stay within the limits of your visa’s work permissions (such as on-campus employment or CPT/OPT for eligible students).

### Work Visas (H-1B, L-1, O-1, etc.)

For Indian professionals seeking employment in the U.S., the situation has become more complex. H-1B visa applicants, in particular, face increased scrutiny, as immigration authorities are verifying employer-employee relationships, job offer legitimacy, and wage compliance.

**Key points to keep in mind:**  
Ensure your employer follows immigration laws and provides complete documentation. Be prepared for site visits and additional scrutiny regarding your job role and work location. Stay updated on visa extension policies and rule changes.

### Family and Spouse Visas (H-4, K-1, IR-1, etc.)

Indian nationals applying for dependent or spousal visas must also be cautious. H-4 visa holders, particularly those seeking work authorization (H-4 EAD), may experience delays. Spouse visa applicants should be ready to provide extensive proof of relationship to avoid fraud-related denials.

**To ensure a smooth process:**  
Maintain all necessary documentation proving relationship legitimacy. Follow up on visa status regularly. Seek legal advice for prolonged delays or unexpected denials.

---

## Increased Risk of Deportation for Visa Violations

A major consequence of these immigration raids is the heightened risk of deportation for those found violating visa terms. Previously, minor infractions might have been overlooked, but now even small issues—such as overstaying a visa by a few days—can lead to serious consequences.

Indian nationals currently in the U.S. should regularly check visa expiration dates and apply for extensions well in advance. Avoid unauthorized employment or activities outside visa permissions. Carry copies of key documents (passport, I-94, visa approval notices) to prove legal status if questioned.

---

## Stricter Background Checks and Delays

In light of increased enforcement, U.S. immigration authorities have ramped up background checks for visa applicants. This has resulted in longer processing times for verification and security clearance.

Applicants should apply early to account for possible delays. Provide complete documentation to minimize back-and-forth communication with immigration officers. Be prepared for additional questions during visa interviews and ensure consistency in responses.

---

## Advice for Indian Visa Applicants

Given the current situation, Indian visa applicants should adopt a proactive approach to ensure their immigration process remains smooth.

### Stay Updated on Policy Changes

Immigration policies in the U.S. are constantly evolving. Tracking new regulations, executive orders, and USCIS announcements helps applicants navigate effectively. Reliable sources include the **U.S. Department of State** and the **USCIS website**.

### Seek Legal Assistance if Needed

For those facing complications, consulting an immigration attorney can be invaluable. Professional guidance helps avoid pitfalls and strengthens applications.

### Ensure Accuracy in Applications

Even simple errors in paperwork can cause rejections or delays. Double-check all documents, provide truthful responses during interviews, and retain copies of all submissions.

---

## Conclusion

The recent immigration raids in the U.S. have intensified scrutiny on visa applicants, making it more critical than ever for Indian nationals to follow proper procedures and stay compliant with immigration laws. Whether you are a student, a professional, or a family member applying for a visa, being well-informed and proactive is key to navigating the evolving immigration landscape. By staying updated, preparing thoroughly, and seeking legal guidance when necessary, applicants can improve their chances of a successful and hassle-free immigration experience.
