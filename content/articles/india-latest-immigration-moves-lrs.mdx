# Unlocking Opportunities: India’s Latest Immigration Moves

**Written by Huma Siddiqui**  
**Published on:** March 31, 2024 | 13:36 IST  

---

India’s recent adjustments to its immigration policies, particularly concerning the **Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)**, have sparked discussions among experts and professionals alike.

---

## Understanding the Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)

The **LRS**, introduced by the **Reserve Bank of India (RBI)** in 2004, aims to simplify foreign fund transfers, easing restrictions set by the *Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999*.  

Under this scheme, residents can remit funds up to a specified limit for various permissible transactions, bridging the gap for Indians seeking international opportunities.

---

## Expert Insight: Varun Singh on the Policy Shift

**Varun Singh**, MD of **XIPHIAS Immigration**, sheds light on the significance of these changes, especially during crucial months like **February and April**, which are pivotal for business transactions.

> “With over 6000 families our company has assisted in migration, as it caters to a wide spectrum of clientele, from skilled professionals to High-Net-Worth Individuals (HNIs), facilitating pathways such as golden visas and EB-5 investments.”

Singh elaborates further:  
> “In the realm of technology, our company unveils tailored immigration programs for tech professionals. With an eye on the global talent pool, we have highlighted avenues like **Australia’s Global Talent Visa (GTV)** and the **UK’s Global Talent Immigration (GTI)** program, opening doors for senior management professionals with lucrative opportunities abroad.”

Adding to that, he notes:  
> “Moreover, the government’s exploration of **Mobility Agreements** with various nations presents a promising avenue. XIPHIAS, with its global expertise and robust platform, stands ready to leverage these agreements, facilitating seamless transitions for IT and ITES companies and handling a multitude of applications efficiently.”

Singh emphasizes the broader mission of his company:  
> “Recognizing the dual importance of meeting labour demands worldwide and catering to individuals seeking better prospects, we go beyond merely filling vacancies and empower individuals to pursue their dreams of a better life elsewhere.”

---

## Commitment to Global Citizenship

Since its inception, Singh states that his company has remained committed to exceeding client expectations and guiding individuals towards global citizenship.

> “By demystifying the immigration process, XIPHIAS ensures that no one feels left out due to lack of information, thereby unlocking a world of opportunities for those with the courage to pursue them,” he adds.
