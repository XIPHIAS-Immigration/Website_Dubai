# France’s Startup Visa: A Boost for Entrepreneurial Spirit

France's start-up visa programme to allow international start-up founders to obtain a multi-year residence permit.  
**Written by Huma Siddiqui**  
**July 24, 2024 | 19:15 IST**

---

France has long been a hub for innovation and entrepreneurship, and its startup visa program is designed to attract and support international talent. The **French Tech Visa for Founders** is a simplified procedure that allows international start-up founders to obtain a multi-year residence permit, known as the “Talent Passport.”

> “The French startup visa program transforms the entrepreneurial landscape by providing a simplified and structured process for establishing a business in France. With its robust economy, business-friendly environment, and exceptional quality of life, France is an alluring destination for international talent. This visa program plays a crucial role in making France an even more attractive option for entrepreneurs, fostering innovation and growth through a streamlined pathway to setting up a business in the country,”  
> — **Varun Singh, MD, XIPHIAS Immigration**, explains to *FinancialExpress.com.*

According to Varun Singh,  
“This visa is dedicated to the tech ecosystem and is available for three types of international tech talent: founders, employees, and investors. To qualify, founders must provide an innovative business project recognised by a public-sector body and demonstrate sufficient annual financial means, equivalent to the statutory national minimum wage,” Varun Singh, MD, XIPHIAS Immigration, explains to *FinancialExpress.com.*

## Following are excerpts from an exclusive interaction:

### What are the benefits this Startup Visa offers?

The French startup visa program offers numerous benefits, including the ability to bring family members and participate in commercial, industrial, or agricultural activities. The program is designed to attract international talent and encourage economic activity, making it an attractive option for entrepreneurs looking to break into the French and European markets.

The visa process is streamlined, with key steps including application to a **French Tech Visa partner incubator or accelerator**, recognition of the innovative nature of the project, and submission of the application for a **long-stay visa marked “Talent Passport.”**

Overall, the French startup visa program is a significant boost for entrepreneurial spirit, providing a supportive environment for international entrepreneurs to establish and grow their businesses in France.

---

### Duration of the visa?

The visa allows for a **maximum duration of four years**, renewable on a yearly basis, and provides a streamlined process for obtaining a residence permit. This visa is particularly beneficial for entrepreneurs who want to establish their business in France and contribute to the country’s thriving startup scene.

## The EU’s Blue Card: A Path to Permanent Residency for Skilled Workers

The **European Union’s Blue Card** is a residence permit designed for highly skilled workers from non-EU countries. This program aims to attract and retain top talent in the EU, addressing the region’s labour shortages and demographic challenges.

To be eligible, applicants must hold a **university degree** and have **at least five years of relevant work experience**. They must also have a job offer in the EU that meets specific salary and qualification requirements. The Blue Card offers a range of benefits, including the right to work and live in the EU, access to social security and healthcare, and the possibility of **family reunification.**

---

### Is the Blue Card a step closer to permanent residency?

Yes. The Blue Card is a significant step towards **permanent residency in the EU.** After four years of continuous residence, Blue Card holders can apply for a **permanent residence permit**, which grants them the right to live and work in the EU indefinitely.

This path to permanent residency is particularly appealing to skilled workers who want to establish a long-term career in the EU. The Blue Card program has been successful in attracting highly qualified professionals, particularly in fields such as technology, healthcare, and finance. As the EU continues to face labour shortages and demographic challenges, the Blue Card remains an important tool for attracting and retaining top talent, driving economic growth, and ensuring the long-term competitiveness of the region.

> “The EU Blue Card is a valuable tool for highly skilled workers seeking to establish a long-term career in the EU. As an immigration consultant, I have seen firsthand the benefits it offers, particularly in terms of increased mobility and uninterrupted stay.  
> For instance, a client of mine, a highly qualified IT professional, was able to relocate to multiple EU countries without losing the accumulated years of stay, which significantly enhanced his career prospects. This flexibility is a major draw for ambitious individuals seeking to build a life in the EU.”
