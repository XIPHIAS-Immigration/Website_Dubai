## Schengen Visa for Families: Tips for Stress-Free Travel With Kids

**Curated By:** Swati Chaturvedi, *News18.com*  
**Last Updated:** December 05, 2024, 13:39 IST

Traveling with kids to Schengen countries can be a deeply rewarding adventure when approached with preparation and patience.

Traveling to Schengen countries with your family can be a truly enriching experience, offering opportunities to create lasting memories. However, the visa process requires careful planning, especially when traveling with children. Here are expert insights to help make your journey smoother.

---

## Start with Proper Planning and Documentation

**Shikhar Aggarwal**, Joint Managing Director, **BLS International**, emphasizes that families should begin their visa applications well in advance, especially during peak travel seasons.  

> “Each family member, including infants, must have a valid passport and an individual visa application as required by Schengen regulations. Assemble all necessary documents, including proof of relationship like birth certificates for children and family travel insurance," he advises.

A detailed itinerary with confirmed accommodations and return tickets strengthens the application.

Echoing this sentiment, **Mohak Nahta**, CEO & Founder, **Atlys**, adds:  

> “Comprehensive documentation is crucial. Incomplete paperwork is a leading cause of rejection, contributing to 42% of failed applications according to Atlys data. For children, ensure you include parental consent forms, school leave letters, and proof of dependency."

---

## Timing and Group Applications Matter

Timing is essential for a smooth process. **Nahta** recommends applying at least **4–6 weeks** before your intended travel date to accommodate any processing delays.  

> “Group applications are advantageous. Processing all family members together simplifies tracking and reduces discrepancies," he notes.

**Aggarwal** also stresses patience, noting that visa processing and navigating airports with children require flexibility and a positive attitude.  

> “This approach ensures a stress-free experience and allows families to focus on the joys of travel," he says.

---

## Consider Long-Term Solutions for Frequent Travelers

For families looking beyond short-term travel, **Varun Singh**, Managing Director of **XIPHIAS Immigration**, suggests exploring options like **Golden Visas, Residency Visas**, or **Citizenship-by-Investment** programs.  

> “While the Schengen Visa provides access to 27 European countries, it comes with limitations such as validity periods and renewal processes. Permanent residency or second citizenship through investment programs eliminates these challenges, offering visa-free access and flexibility," Singh explains.

These programs also provide benefits like access to superior healthcare and education, making them ideal for frequent travelers.

---

## Strengthen Your Application with Family-Friendly Details

When crafting your application, focus on showcasing your family-oriented plans. **Nahta** advises:  

> “Highlight family-friendly accommodations and activities in your itinerary. Pair this with robust financial evidence, such as recent bank statements and proof of income, to demonstrate stability."

Most consulates expect families to meet a daily expense threshold of **€30–€50 per person**.

Additionally, **Singh** recommends verifying country-specific requirements, as some Schengen nations may request notarized documents for minors or proof of internal travel.

---

## Prepare for the Journey

Beyond the paperwork, packing smart can significantly enhance the travel experience. **Aggarwal** suggests carrying essentials like snacks, entertainment, and comfort items for kids, especially for long journeys.  

Researching **child-friendly attractions and services** at your destination is another way to ensure everyone enjoys the trip.

> “Being familiar with local transportation and healthcare facilities is equally important to prepare for any emergencies," Aggarwal adds.
