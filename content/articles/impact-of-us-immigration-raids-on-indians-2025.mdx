## Impact of US immigration raids on Indians: What visa applicants need to know

Many Indians planning to relocate to the United States have been left anxious after Donald Trump's crackdown on illegals. As deportation of undocumented immigrants continues, here's how people applying for work visas can avoid complications.

The United States government, under the Donald Trump administration, launched a massive crackdown on illegal immigrants, deporting thousands of undocumented immigrants back to their countries. A flight with **109 Indians without documents** landed in Amritsar, and two other flights are expected to land in the next two days. The deportation of immigrants has raised concerns among people, leaving thousands anxious.

Even as India has agreed to take back all verified deportees, there are concerns among those who are in the US or are pursuing avenues for immigration. The US immigration system continues to be one of the most sought-after globally, and while there are challenges, opportunities for lawful immigration still exist.

Varun Singh, Managing Director of **XIPHIAS Immigration**, one of India’s leading global immigration consultancies, recognised that the current climate can be unsettling for many applicants, both inside and outside the US. However, he noted that *“it’s crucial to understand that they do not target those pursuing legitimate legal avenues for immigration.”*

> “For applicants in the US, it is essential to ensure that your visa status and documentation are in full compliance with US immigration law. This includes making sure that your visa has not expired and that you are adhering to the specific terms of your stay. Non-compliance can lead to severe consequences, including deportation. If you are uncertain about your status or your next steps, it is strongly recommended to consult with an immigration expert immediately to evaluate your situation,” Varun Singh said while speaking to *India Today*.

People who are pursuing lawful immigration options have nothing to worry about as the **US Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS)** and other agencies are still processing legitimate applications. For such people, there are established legal pathways for those wishing to come to the US to work, study, or invest.

Given the change in administration in the US and the sweeping announcements that the Trump administration continues to make, people are advised to stay updated on any changes to immigration law or policy to avoid complications.

> “In our experience, staying ahead of changes, ensuring that all documentation is accurate, and seeking professional guidance are the best ways to avoid complications,” Singh said.

## What will be the impact?

The outcome of the **2024 US presidential election** led to significant shifts in immigration policies, with President Donald Trump pledging to initiate the *“largest deportation operation in American history.”* Focusing on reversing migratory trends observed during the previous administration, Trump has already started implementing it. Thousands of people have been deported to their countries so far.

As far as India is concerned, **104 deportees from Haryana and Punjab** landed in India on February 5, with the Centre revealing that **487 illegal Indian migrants** living in the US will be deported soon. On **February 15**, a special flight will land in Amritsar with 119 passengers, sources told *India Today*. A third flight is likely to land on February 16, and arrangements are being made by airport authorities and local administration. The previous landing in Amritsar sparked a political controversy and left many immigrants anxious.

Key appointments, such as **Tom Homan** as the top border official, underscore this commitment to expedited deportations, particularly targeting undocumented immigrants with criminal records.

> “Additionally, a bipartisan group of US senators is engaging in discreet discussions to create a border deal set for early 2025, motivated by the 2024 electoral loss of the Democratic Party. The discussions aim to secure the required 60 Senate votes to pass the bill. Central to the conversation is how to resolve the ongoing illegal immigration crisis, an issue spotlighted during President-elect Donald Trump's campaign, where he pledged strict border actions including deportation of millions of undocumented migrants and border closure,” Varun Singh highlighted.

## Future of work visa in the US

For those anxious about how the latest immigration policies would impact future applications for work visas, the **XIPHIAS Immigration** MD noted that they may face tighter regulations.

> “In the United States, the incoming administration's stringent immigration stance may lead to tighter regulations and increased scrutiny for work visa applicants. Businesses that rely on immigrant labour are expressing concerns over potential workforce shortages and the complexities of navigating the evolving immigration landscape,” he noted.

Meanwhile, **Prime Minister Narendra Modi**, during a press conference in the US on February 14, reiterated that India is ready to take back verified illegal immigrants living abroad. He also highlighted that there is a need to end the entire ecosystem of human trafficking, noting that these individuals are ordinary people who are targeted and shown big dreams.
