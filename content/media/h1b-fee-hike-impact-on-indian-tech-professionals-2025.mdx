## H-1B Fee Hike: Indian Tech Professionals and Students Face Rising Costs Under New U.S. Policies

The recent **increase in H-1B visa fees** and tighter immigration measures introduced by the Trump administration have raised serious concerns among **Indian technology professionals and students** aspiring to work in the United States.  

According to experts featured in a recent panel discussion, these policy shifts are expected to deliver **financial and psychological setbacks** for new applicants. With **Indians forming the largest share of H-1B holders**, the impact will be deeply felt across the tech and education sectors.

The conversation highlighted that the new visa fees will not only burden foreign applicants but also **raise operational costs for American companies**, potentially reducing competitiveness and innovation. Several experts described this move as **counterproductive to U.S. economic interests**, warning that it could lead to a **talent outflow to other countries**.

## Discussion Highlights

- **Indian tech professionals and students** face significant financial pressure due to increased H-1B visa application costs and stricter eligibility rules.  
- The policy change could **impact U.S. companies**, particularly in the technology and startup sectors that rely heavily on skilled foreign workers.  
- Experts suggest that countries like **Canada, Australia, and the U.K.** could attract more skilled talent as alternatives to the U.S.  
- Analysts warn that **Indian students** should reconsider taking large education loans for U.S. programs given the uncertain immigration outlook.  
- The panel emphasized that **long-term consequences** could include a decline in U.S. innovation and global competitiveness.
