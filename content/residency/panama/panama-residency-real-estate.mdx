## Overview
By investing at least **USD 300,000** in qualified real estate in Panama, foreign investors can obtain **permanent residency** through the country’s **Qualified Investor Permanent Residency** route.

This route is designed for applicants seeking a stable, business-friendly jurisdiction with flexible presence requirements and a clear path to long-term status.

## Investment Overview
Applicants must invest at least **USD 300,000** in qualifying real estate. The investment should be maintained for the required period (commonly **5 years**).

You must also demonstrate that the investment funds come from a legitimate source and provide the required supporting documents.

## Key Benefits
- Permanent residency option with a qualifying real estate investment.
- **Family inclusion**: spouse, children, and dependent parents (subject to dependency rules).
- **Foreign-sourced income is generally tax-free** under Panama’s territorial tax system.
- Opportunity to **rent out** the investment property.
- Eligible to apply for **Panamanian citizenship after 5 years** of residency (subject to legal requirements).

## Requirements
Applicants must:
- Be at least **18 years old** with a clean criminal background.
- Make and maintain the **qualifying real estate investment**.
- Hold the investment for the required period (commonly **5 years**).
- Submit required documents including passport, police clearance, health certificate, and proof of investment.
- Pay required government fees.

## Document Checklist
Basic documents required:
- Valid passport with a minimum validity of at least **6 months**.
- Completed application forms (including personal history/background declaration).
- Passport-size photos.
- Police clearance certificate from the country of origin or residence; valid, apostilled, or authenticated.
- Certificate of good health.
- Proof of investment and proof of lawful source of funds.
- Proof of payment of government fees.

If applying with dependents: documents proving relationship (marriage certificate for spouse, birth certificates for children) and dependency evidence where applicable.

## Risks and Compliance
**Risks:**
- Panama’s investment and immigration laws may be altered.
- Residency status may be impacted if the minimum visit requirements are not met.
- Political and economic volatility may affect investments or ease of doing business.

**Compliance:**
- Demonstrate the investment funds’ **legitimate source**.
- Visit Panama **at least once every two years** to keep permanent residency active.
- Provide accurate information; false claims may result in rejection or revocation.

## Application Process
- **Step 1:** Candidate contacts XIPHIAS for a preliminary eligibility assessment and guidance.
- **Step 2:** XIPHIAS assists the candidate in completing the qualifying investment and submitting the required documentation.
- **Step 3:** Authorized local representatives work with XIPHIAS to submit the application for permanent residency to the Panamanian government.
- **Step 4:** Panamanian officials review and verify the applicant’s investment, funding source, and background.
- **Step 5:** Upon acceptance, the applicant receives a permanent resident card and direct permanent status in Panama.

## FAQs
1. **What is the Immigration Program for Qualified Investors in Panama?**  
   A program granting permanent residency after a qualifying investment (including real estate).

2. **Who qualifies?**  
   Candidates must be at least 18 years old, have no criminal history, be in good health, and make a qualifying investment.

3. **Who qualifies as a dependent?**  
   Typically spouse, dependent children (including certain adult children who are enrolled in school and financially dependent), and dependent parents.

4. **What are the advantages?**  
   Permanent residency with low presence requirements and the ability to maintain status with periodic visits.

5. **What is the duration of the procedure?**  
   Processing commonly takes about 60–90 days, and travel to Panama may be required for photo and fingerprinting.
